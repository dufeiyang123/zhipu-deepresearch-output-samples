# Section 1：章节研究计划

## Chapter 1：Introduction — The Rise of AIGC in K-12 Education

### 研究目标
- Establish clear, citable definitions of AIGC / generative AI in the education context (academic literature, UNESCO/OECD definitions).
- Compile a verified timeline of major AIGC-in-education milestones (tool launches, policy releases, enrollment figures) from 2022 to April 2026.
- Identify and justify a dual-axis taxonomic framework (pedagogical function × stakeholder role) by reviewing existing classification schemes.
- Locate aggregate adoption statistics: what percentage of K-12 teachers/students report using generative AI tools (RAND, OECD, Stanford HAI, national surveys).

### 关键发现

**A. Definitions**
- UNESCO defines Generative AI as "an AI technology that automatically generates content in response to prompts written in natural language conversational interfaces," producing "texts, images, videos, music and software code." [UNESCO Guidance for GenAI in Education](https://table.media/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/386693eng.pdf "UNESCO, Miao & Holmes, September 2023, Section 1.1")
- The term "AIGC" is functionally equivalent to GenAI outputs in Chinese-language policy discourse; China's MOE defines GenAI as "models and related technology that have the ability to generate text, graphics, audio, video, and other content." [China MOE Guide (2025)](https://cset.georgetown.edu/publication/china-school-gen-ai-use-guide-2025/ "Georgetown CSET English translation of China MOE GenAI school guide, 2025 Edition")
- UNESCO distinguishes GenAI from earlier AIED paradigms (adaptive learning, ITS, learning analytics): GenAI produces *new content* rather than merely analyzing data or providing pre-authored responses. UNESCO also introduces the concept of "EdGPT"—foundation models fine-tuned for education (e.g., EduChat, MathGPT). [UNESCO Guidance for GenAI in Education](https://table.media/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/386693eng.pdf "UNESCO 2023, Section 1.4")

**B. Timeline of Key Milestones (Nov 2022 – Apr 2026)**
- Nov 30, 2022: OpenAI launches ChatGPT (GPT-3.5); reaches 100M MAU by Jan 2023.
- Jan 2023: NYC public schools ban ChatGPT; debate between "ban" vs. "teach with it" begins globally.
- Mar 14, 2023: GPT-4 released; Khanmigo launched (GPT-4-powered AI tutor) in limited pilot. [Khan Academy Blog](https://blog.khanacademy.org/khanmigo-top-ai-in-education-moments-of-2023-the-year-artificial-intelligence-dominated-education-news-kl/ "Khan Academy year-in-review 2023")
- May 2023: UNESCO convenes first global ministerial round table on GenAI in education; U.S. DOE publishes "AI and the Future of Teaching and Learning." [USDOE](https://www.ed.gov/sites/ed/files/documents/ai-report/ai-report.pdf "USDOE AI report, May 2023")
- Jul 13, 2023: China becomes first country to regulate GenAI (Provisional Regulations on GenAI Services).
- Sep 7, 2023: UNESCO publishes "Guidance for Generative AI in Education and Research"—first global GenAI-in-education guidance. [UNESCO](https://www.unesco.org/en/articles/guidance-generative-ai-education-and-research "UNESCO, Sep 2023")
- Feb 2, 2024: EU AI Act voted; classifies education AI as "high-risk"; enters force Aug 1, 2024. [Kennedys Law](https://www.kennedyslaw.com/en/thought-leadership/article/2026/the-eu-ai-act-implementation-timeline-understanding-the-next-deadline-for-compliance/ "EU AI Act timeline")
- Fall 2024: U.S. teen ChatGPT usage for schoolwork doubles from 13% (2023) to 26% (n=1,391). [Pew Research](https://www.pewresearch.org/short-reads/2025/01/15/about-a-quarter-of-us-teens-have-used-chatgpt-for-schoolwork-double-the-share-in-2023/ "Pew, Jan 2025")
- Dec 2024: China MOE designates 184 primary/secondary schools as AI education bases. [IEEE IT Professional](https://www.computer.org/csdl/magazine/it/2025/05/11218250/2b9Tuwsg7aU "IEEE 2025")
- 2024–2025: Khanmigo expands to 2 million students/teachers (US, India, Brazil); Microsoft partnership makes it free for U.S. teachers. [Microsoft](https://news.microsoft.com/source/features/ai/khan-academy-and-microsoft-partner-to-expand-access-to-ai-tools/ "Microsoft-Khan Academy partnership")
- May 2025: China MOE releases "Guide to Using GenAI in Primary and Secondary Schools (2025 Edition)." [Georgetown CSET](https://cset.georgetown.edu/publication/china-school-gen-ai-use-guide-2025/ "CSET translation, May 13, 2025")
- Jul 2025: U.S. DOE issues guidance on AI in schools and proposes supplemental grantmaking priority on AI. [USDOE](https://www.ed.gov/about/news/press-release/us-department-of-education-issues-guidance-artificial-intelligence-use-schools-proposes-additional-supplemental-priority "USDOE press release, 2025")
- Sep 1, 2025: AI education becomes mandatory across all Chinese primary/secondary schools.
- Sep 30, 2025: RAND publishes major update "AI Use in Schools Is Quickly Increasing but Guidance Lags Behind." [RAND](https://www.rand.org/pubs/research_reports/RRA4180-1.html "RAND RR-A4180-1, Doss et al., Sep 2025")
- Oct 2025: OECD publishes TALIS 2024 results (≈41% of OECD teachers use AI). [Education International](https://www.ei-ie.org/en/item/31815:using-artificial-intelligence-takeaways-from-talis-2024 "EI analysis of TALIS 2024, Dec 2025")
- Jan 2026: OECD publishes "Digital Education Outlook 2026: Exploring Effective Uses of GenAI in Education." [OECD](https://www.oecd.org/en/blogs/2026/01/how-to-effectively-use-generative-ai-in-education.html "Schleicher blog, Jan 19, 2026")

**C. Adoption Statistics**
- U.S. K-12 teachers (Fall 2023): 18% regularly using AI, 15% tried at least once (~33% total). [RAND](https://www.rand.org/pubs/research_reports/RRA956-21.html "RAND 2024, Fall 2023 data")
- U.S. K-12 teachers (Spring 2025): 53% of ELA/math/science teachers use AI for school (+15pp vs. prior years). [RAND](https://www.rand.org/pubs/research_reports/RRA4180-1.html "RAND, Doss et al. 2025")
- U.S. K-12 students (2025): 54% use AI for school (+15pp increase). [RAND](https://www.rand.org/pubs/research_reports/RRA4180-1.html "RAND 2025")
- U.S. teens (Fall 2025): 54% used AI chatbots for schoolwork help; 10% do all/most schoolwork with chatbot help (n=1,458). [Pew Research](https://www.pewresearch.org/internet/2026/02/24/how-teens-use-and-view-ai/ "Pew, Feb 2026")
- OECD teachers (TALIS 2024): 37–41% of lower-secondary teachers use AI, ranging from 75% (UAE, Singapore) to 14% (France); ~300,000 teachers in 50+ systems surveyed. Most common use: learn about/summarize topics (68%), generate lesson plans (64%). Least common: assessing student work (26%). Non-user barrier: 75% cite lack of skills. [Education International / OECD](https://www.ei-ie.org/en/item/31815:using-artificial-intelligence-takeaways-from-talis-2024 "EI/OECD TALIS 2024")
- Swiss students (2024, n=10,000, ages 8–18): Regular classroom GenAI use: 8% primary, 30% lower-secondary, 50% upper-secondary, 42% vocational. Home use for schoolwork follows similar pattern. [OECD AI Adoption Report](https://www.oecd.org/content/dam/oecd/en/publications/reports/2025/12/ai-adoption-in-the-education-system_43251cf0/69bd0a4a-en.pdf "OECD 2025, citing Oggenfuss & Wolter 2024")
- OECD policy survey (2025): 81% of 32 responding jurisdictions mention AI in education strategies, but only 25% have specific AI initiatives; only 19% include timebound goals. [OECD AI Adoption Report](https://www.oecd.org/content/dam/oecd/en/publications/reports/2025/12/ai-adoption-in-the-education-system_43251cf0/69bd0a4a-en.pdf "OECD 2025")
- U.S. district policy lag (Spring 2025): Only 45% of principals report having AI policies; only 34% of teachers report policies on AI and academic integrity; over 80% of students say teachers did not teach them how to use AI. [RAND](https://www.rand.org/pubs/research_reports/RRA4180-1.html "RAND 2025")
- OECD RCT evidence (Türkiye, n=839 secondary students): Standard ChatGPT improved practice performance by 48% but *decreased* post-test performance by 17%; a structured ChatGPT Tutor with pedagogical safeguards improved practice by 127% with no post-test decline—underscoring purpose-built education AI vs. general-purpose tools. [OECD AI Adoption Report](https://www.oecd.org/content/dam/oecd/en/publications/reports/2025/12/ai-adoption-in-the-education-system_43251cf0/69bd0a4a-en.pdf "OECD 2025, citing Bastani et al. 2024")
- Concern data: 61% of U.S. parents, 48% of middle schoolers, 55% of high schoolers worry AI will harm critical-thinking skills; only 22% of district leaders share this concern. 50% of students worry about being falsely accused of using AI to cheat. [RAND](https://www.rand.org/pubs/research_reports/RRA4180-1.html "RAND 2025")

**D. Taxonomy Frameworks**
- Zawacki-Richter et al. (2019): four categories—profiling/prediction, ITS, assessment/evaluation, adaptive systems/personalization. Pre-GenAI, data-driven focus. [Zawacki-Richter et al.](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1186/s41239-019-0171-0 "IJETHE Vol. 16, 2019")
- Holmes, Bialik & Fadel (2019): three-way stakeholder taxonomy—student-facing AI, teacher-facing AI, system-facing AI. Provides precedent for stakeholder axis of the dual-axis framework. [Holmes et al.](https://curriculumredesign.org/wp-content/uploads/AIED-Book-Excerpt-CCR.pdf "CCR 2019, book excerpt")
- Holmes & Tuomi (2022): updated AIED taxonomy discussing emergence of generative models; categorizes by pedagogical function (tutoring, assessment, content generation, admin support). [Holmes & Tuomi](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1111/ejed.12533 "European Journal of Education Vol. 57, 2022")
- UNESCO (2023): five functional co-design categories—research, teacher support, 1:1 coaching for foundational skills, inquiry/project-based learning, learners with special needs. [UNESCO Guidance](https://table.media/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/386693eng.pdf "UNESCO 2023, Section 5.3")
- OECD Digital Education Outlook 2026: "pedagogical purpose" vs. "general-purpose" distinction as key effectiveness factor. [OECD](https://www.oecd.org/en/blogs/2026/01/how-to-effectively-use-generative-ai-in-education.html "Schleicher blog, Jan 2026")
- The report's proposed dual-axis framework (pedagogical function × stakeholder role) synthesizes Holmes et al.'s stakeholder axis with UNESCO/Zawacki-Richter functional categorization; no prior publication uses this exact formulation, but both constituent dimensions are well-established.

## Chapter 2：The Global Policy and Institutional Landscape

### 研究目标
- Obtain and analyze key policy documents: UNESCO GenAI guidance (2023, any 2025 updates), UNESCO AI Competency Framework for Teachers, OECD Digital Education Outlook, China MOE guide on GenAI in primary/secondary schools (2025), U.S. DOE AI guidance, UK DfE AI framework, Singapore MOE AI strategy, Japan MEXT policies, South Korea MOE digital textbook/AI policies, Australia's national AI-in-schools framework.
- Identify at least 8 countries' official positions on student-facing generative AI use in K-12.
- Map policy dimensions: permitted age ranges, data residency requirements, teacher PD mandates, content filtering requirements.
- Conduct comparative analysis to find convergences, divergences, and regulatory gaps.

### 关键发现

**A. Supranational Frameworks**
- UNESCO Guidance for GenAI in Education (Sep 2023): proposes a seven-step regulatory framework for governments; calls for age 13 minimum for GenAI use. [UNESCO Guidance](https://table.media/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/386693eng.pdf "UNESCO, Miao & Holmes, September 2023")
- UNESCO AI Competency Framework for Teachers (Aug 2024): defines 15 competencies across 5 dimensions (human-centred mindset, ethics, AI foundations, AI pedagogy, professional learning), with 3 progression levels (Acquire, Deepen, Create). [UNESCO AI CFT](https://www.unesco.org/en/articles/ai-competency-framework-teachers "UNESCO, August 2024")
- OECD Digital Education Outlook 2023, Ch 10: among 18 countries, zero had enacted specific binding GenAI-in-education regulation as of early 2024; 9 published non-binding guidance, 9 delegated to schools, 7 discouraged GenAI below certain age (typically 13). [OECD DEO 2023](https://www.oecd.org/en/publications/oecd-digital-education-outlook-2023_c74f03de-en/full-report/emerging-governance-of-generative-ai-in-education_3cbd6269.html "OECD, Dec 2023")
- OECD Jan 2026 analysis: purpose-built education AI tools deliver stronger outcomes than general-purpose chatbots; policy frameworks should distinguish between the two. [OECD Blog](https://www.oecd.org/en/blogs/2026/01/how-to-effectively-use-generative-ai-in-education.html "OECD, Jan 2026")
- EU AI Act (entered force Aug 1, 2024): education AI classified as high-risk under Annex III Section 3, covering four use cases (admission decisions, learning outcome evaluation, education level assessment, student behavior monitoring during tests). High-risk provisions take full effect Aug 2, 2026. [EU AI Act Annex III](https://artificialintelligenceact.eu/annex/3/ "EU AI Act official text")

**B. Americas and Europe**
- U.S. (Trump administration): Apr 23, 2025 Executive Order "Advancing AI Education for American Youth" — White House Task Force, public-private partnerships, Presidential AI Challenge; Jul 22, 2025 Dear Colleague Letter on federal funds for AI in education (three areas: instructional materials, tutoring, career pathways; five principles: educator-led, ethical, accessible, transparent, data-protective); Dec 11, 2025 EO to preempt state AI regulations (120+ AI-in-education bills in state legislatures in 2025; Ohio and Tennessee require district AI policies). [White House](https://www.whitehouse.gov/presidential-actions/2025/04/advancing-artificial-intelligence-education-for-american-youth/ "EO, Apr 2025"); [WCET](https://wcet.wiche.edu/frontiers/2025/08/11/understanding-the-trump-administrations-educational-ai-policies/ "WCET analysis, Aug 2025"); [EdWeek](https://www.edweek.org/technology/k-12-world-reacts-to-trumps-executive-order-to-block-state-ai-regulations/2025/12 "EdWeek, Dec 2025")
- UK DfE: Oct 2023 policy paper (updated 2025), permissive/pro-innovation stance; £4M in AI tools, £2M for Oak National Academy's "Aila" lesson assistant (saves teachers 3–4 hrs/week), £45M digital connectivity; school-level autonomy within GDPR and "Keeping Children Safe in Education." [UK DfE Education Hub](https://educationhub.blog.gov.uk/2025/06/artificial-intelligence-in-schools-everything-you-need-to-know/ "DfE, June 2025")
- Canada: Provincial fragmentation — only 3 of 13 provinces with published K-12 AI guidance (BC 2023, New Brunswick Jul 2024, Quebec Nov 2024; Quebec suspended AI assistants Mar 2025). Federal AIDA does not address K-12 specifically. [EdCan Report](https://www.edcan.ca/wp-content/uploads/Ai-in-K-12-Frameworks.pdf "EdCan, Dec 2025")
- Europe: Austria published non-binding guidance; Finland leaves to schools, published AI Guide for Teachers (2024); France drafted guidance with age restriction; Netherlands created National Education Lab AI (NOLAI); European Commission published "Guidelines on ethical use of AI and data in teaching and learning for educators." [OECD DEO 2023](https://www.oecd.org/en/publications/oecd-digital-education-outlook-2023_c74f03de-en/full-report/emerging-governance-of-generative-ai-in-education_3cbd6269.html "OECD, Dec 2023")

**C. East Asia, Southeast Asia, and Oceania**
- China MOE Guide (May 13, 2025): most detailed national GenAI-in-K-12 guidance globally. Age-differentiated thresholds (primary: banned from independent open-ended AI use; middle: may explore logical structure; high school: inquiry-based AI learning). Students banned from submitting AI-generated work as original. AI plays "supplementary role" only; direct use for evaluating students/answering exams/processing sensitive data prohibited. Localized management policies, ethical review, dynamic "whitelists" required. Tiered AI education progression (basic cognitive → deeper analysis → applied innovation). [Georgetown CSET](https://cset.georgetown.edu/publication/china-school-gen-ai-use-guide-2025/ "CSET translation, May 2025"); [China Daily](https://www.chinadaily.com.cn/a/202505/16/WS6826a712a310a04af22bfd31.html "May 16, 2025")
- China: mandatory AI education across all primary/secondary schools from Sep 1, 2025; 184 schools as AI education bases (Dec 2024). [IEEE IT Professional](https://www.computer.org/csdl/magazine/it/2025/05/11218250/2b9Tuwsg7aU "IEEE 2025")
- Singapore MOE (EdTech Masterplan 2030): four-tier approach (learn about AI, learn to use AI, learn with AI, learn beyond AI). Government-built SLS platform with Teaching/Feedback/Learning/Adaptive Learning Assistants; ALS supports P5–S2 Math and Upper Secondary Geography. AI-in-Education Ethics Framework (Agency, Inclusivity, Fairness, Safety). [Singapore MOE](https://www.moe.gov.sg/education-in-sg/educational-technology-journey/edtech-masterplan/artificial-intelligence-in-education "MOE, Jan 2026")
- Japan MEXT: guidelines v1.0 (Jul 2023, one of earliest countries), v2.0 (Apr 2025). "Human-centred utilization" — AI as "one of the references." Consider developmental stage. Combined approach: learning about AI, learning to use it, actively using it. GenAI Pilot Schools since FY2023. Mar 2026: 220 high school textbooks incorporating GenAI content passed screening. [MEXT](https://www.mext.go.jp/content/20250422-mxt_shuukyo01-000030823_001.pdf "MEXT v2.0, Apr 2025"); [Japan Times](https://www.japantimes.co.jp/news/2026/03/25/japan/high-school-textbooks-ai/ "Mar 2026")
- South Korea: $850M AI digital textbook program (76 textbooks for math/English/informatics, grades 3–4/middle 1/high 1, launched Mar 2025). Stripped of official textbook status after 4 months (Aug 2025) due to factual inaccuracies, privacy risks, increased screen time, teacher workload complaints. Adoption dropped from 37% to 19% of schools. Failure attributed to rushed timeline (12 vs. standard 18 months), lack of teacher/parent input, political turmoil. [Rest of World](https://restofworld.org/2025/south-korea-ai-textbook/ "Oct 15, 2025")
- Australia: National Framework for Generative AI in Schools (Nov 2023, reviewed Jun 2025). 6 principles, 25 guiding statements. National AI in Schools Taskforce. Dec 2025 National AI Plan with school implications. State supplementary policies (e.g., Victoria 2024). [Australian DOE](https://www.education.gov.au/schooling/resources/australian-framework-generative-artificial-intelligence-ai-schools "Nov 2023, modified Jun 2025")

**D. Comparative Analysis**
- Permissive vs. restrictive: US (most pro-adoption), UK (permissive/pro-innovation) → China (detailed restrictions + mandatory education) → EU (binding high-risk classification from Aug 2026).
- Centralized vs. school-level: China & Singapore most centralized (whitelists, government platforms); UK, Finland, Iceland, Sweden delegate to schools; US most decentralized (50 states, 120+ bills in 2025).
- Age thresholds: China sets graduated thresholds; UNESCO recommends 13; Japan considers "developmental stage" without exact cutoff; 7/18 OECD countries discourage below 13; US/UK no federal age threshold.
- Data privacy: EU AI Act most stringent; China requires whitelists + ethical review; Singapore controls via government platform; US relies on FERPA; Australia principles-based without mandatory compliance.
- Teacher PD: UNESCO 15-competency framework as global reference; US/UK/China/Singapore/Japan all investing in teacher training, but approaches vary from centralized programs to school-level discretion.
- Content filtering: China most restrictive (whitelists, banned AI for grading/exams); Singapore uses government platform guardrails; UK applies "Keeping Children Safe" statutory guidance; US/Australia school-level discretion.
- Early movers: Australia (Nov 2023), Japan (Jul 2023), UK (Oct 2023). South Korea as cautionary tale ($850M failure). US late to federal guidance but aggressive on adoption promotion.
- Key gap: most countries lack binding enforcement—OECD found zero binding regulations in 18 countries as of early 2024; situation evolving (China mandatory 2025, EU binding 2026) but majority still advisory.

## Chapter 3：Taxonomy of AIGC Application Approaches in K-12 Classrooms

### 研究目标
- Review existing academic taxonomies of AI applications in education (e.g., Holmes et al., Zawacki-Richter et al.) and adapt for the generative AI subset.
- For each of five application categories (Content Generation, Personalized Tutoring, Assessment & Feedback, Creative Expression, AI Literacy), identify 3–5 representative tools with documented K-12 use cases.
- Gather evidence on which subject areas and age bands show the highest AIGC adoption density.
- Document the pedagogical rationale and learning theory alignment for each category (Bloom's taxonomy, constructivism, ZPD for AI tutoring, etc.).

### 关键发现

**A. Existing Academic Taxonomies**
- Zawacki-Richter et al. (2019): four categories (profiling/prediction, assessment/evaluation, adaptive systems/personalization, ITS) from 146 articles on AI in higher ed (2007–2018). 63% addressed academic support; only 1.4% addressed ethical challenges. [Zawacki-Richter et al.](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1186/s41239-019-0171-0 "IJETHE, 2019, cited 10,854 times")
- Holmes, Bialik & Fadel (2019): tripartite taxonomy—student-facing, teacher-facing, system-facing. Documents ITS architecture (domain/pedagogical/learner/open learner models). [Holmes et al.](https://curriculumredesign.org/wp-content/uploads/AIED-Book-Excerpt-CCR.pdf "CCR 2019")
- Holmes & Tuomi (2022): updated typology organized around student/teacher/institution axes, explicitly addressing emergence of generative AI; notes expectations "often based on misunderstanding." [Holmes & Tuomi](https://eric.ed.gov/?id=EJ1355026 "EJE v57 n4, Dec 2022")
- Proposed five-category framework maps to prior work: Categories 1 (content generation), 4 (creative expression), and 5 (AI literacy) are distinctly generative-AI phenomena absent from pre-2022 frameworks. Categories 2 and 3 update traditional ITS/assessment categories.
- Alfarwan (2025): systematic review of 30 empirical GenAI K-12 studies—high school students and STEM subjects dominate; ChatGPT used in 21/30; no ECE studies, no Africa representation. [Alfarwan](https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/education/articles/10.3389/feduc.2025.1647573/full "Frontiers in Education, Sep 2025")
- Stanford SCALE (2026): reviewed 800+ papers, identified only 20 high-quality causal studies on AI in K-12. Tool design with pedagogical guardrails consistently outperforms general-purpose chatbots. No causal studies of student AI use in U.S. K-12 classrooms. [Stanford SCALE](https://scale.stanford.edu/research-in-action/understanding-evidence-base-ai-k12-education "March 2026")

**B. Category 1 — Content Generation & Lesson Preparation (Teacher-Facing)**
- MagicSchool AI: 80+ teacher-facing tools (lesson plans, text levelers, rubric creators, worksheets); fewer hallucinations vs. generic ChatGPT; free for individuals. [Edutopia](https://www.edutopia.org/article/4-time-saving-ai-tools-for-teachers/ "Jan 2024")
- Diffit: AI text leveler, translates articles to any reading level (grades 2–11) with vocabulary lists and assessment questions in <30 seconds. [Edutopia](https://www.edutopia.org/article/4-time-saving-ai-tools-for-teachers/ "Jan 2024")
- Curipod: generates full interactive slide presentations (~14 slides) from a single prompt with polls, reflection questions, exit tickets, and auto-grading. [Edutopia](https://www.edutopia.org/article/4-time-saving-ai-tools-for-teachers/ "Jan 2024")
- Oak National Academy Aila (UK): £2M DfE-funded; 10,000+ teachers, 25,000 lesson plans in first two months; 85% rated quality "fairly high" or "very high"; time savings from 30 min to 5–10 min per lesson; nearly two-thirds reported decreased workload. [Oak National Academy](https://www.thenational.academy/blog/how-is-aila-impacting-teacher-lesson-planning-practices-workload-and-expertise-early-insights "Feb 2025")
- EEF trial: teachers using ChatGPT for lesson planning saved average 25 minutes per session. [Oak National Academy](https://www.thenational.academy/blog/how-is-aila-impacting-teacher-lesson-planning-practices-workload-and-expertise-early-insights "Feb 2025, citing EEF")
- TALIS 2024: lesson plan generation is the #1 teacher AI use at 64%; topic summarization at 68%. [Education International/OECD](https://www.ei-ie.org/en/item/31815:using-artificial-intelligence-takeaways-from-talis-2024 "Dec 2025")

**C. Category 2 — Personalized Tutoring & Adaptive Learning (Student-Facing)**
- Khanmigo: GPT-4-powered, Socratic dialogue, ~2M students/teachers (US, India, Brazil). Efficacy data (2022–23, ~350K students grades 3–8): 30+ min/week → ~20% greater-than-expected learning gains on MAP Growth (effect size 0.36). [Khan Academy](https://blog.khanacademy.org/khan-academy-efficacy-results-november-2024/ "Nov 2024")
- Squirrel AI (China): 24M+ students served, "large adaptive model" (LAM), TIME Best Inventions 2025. Personalizes lesson plans/testing, expanding to U.S. market. [TIME](https://time.com/collections/best-inventions-2025/7318298/squirrel-ai-intelligent-adaptive-learning-system/ "Oct 2025")
- Bastani et al. RCT (Türkiye, n≈1,000 HS math students, Fall 2023, PNAS Jun 2025): GPT Base improved practice by 48% but decreased exam performance by 17%; GPT Tutor (with pedagogical guardrails) improved practice by 127% with no exam decline. GPT Base correct only 51% of the time. Students did not perceive learning loss. [Bastani et al.](https://www.pnas.org/doi/10.1073/pnas.2422633122 "PNAS v122 n26, Jun 2025")
- Singapore ALS: government-built adaptive learning for P5–S2 Math and Upper Secondary Geography; LEA asks guiding questions in multiple roles. [Singapore MOE](https://www.moe.gov.sg/education-in-sg/educational-technology-journey/edtech-masterplan/artificial-intelligence-in-education "Jan 2026")

### 研究目标

### 关键发现

**Case Study 1: Khanmigo District Rollout in Newark, NJ — USA**
- Context: Newark Public Schools, 43,000 students, 66 schools (Pre-K–12), historically under-resourced. Piloted 2023 at First Avenue Elementary → expanded to 14 North Ward schools → all 66 schools, reaching ~29,000 students. $25K Gates Foundation grant; 3-year longitudinal study with ~8,000 students. Khanmigo nationally grew from 68K users (2023–24) to 700K+ (2024–25), from 45 to 380+ district partners.
- Outcomes: Students achieving Yearly Proficiency on Khan Academy scored +6 points on NJSLA math (vs. state average +2). Broader efficacy (350K students, 2022–23): 30+ min/week → ~20% greater-than-expected MAP Growth gains (effect size 0.36). [Khan Academy](https://blog.khanacademy.org/khan-academy-efficacy-results-november-2024/ "Nov 2024"); [AEI](https://www.aei.org/op-eds/can-an-ai-powered-tutor-produce-meaningful-results/ "Jul 2025")
- Challenges: Shallow student engagement ("Bro, IDK" responses); students lack metacognitive skills for AI interaction; teachers default to generating MCQs. Solution: 3×5 cards with question stems.
- Takeaway: AI tutoring works best with strong teacher mediation; dosage of 30+ min/week matters; Khanmigo-specific causal data still pending.

**Case Study 2: Bastani et al. RCT — AI Tutoring with/without Guardrails — Türkiye**
- Context: ~1,000 HS math students, large Turkish high school, Fall 2023. Pre-registered, UPenn IRB approved. T1 source (PNAS, Jun 2025).
- Three arms: GPT Base (unguarded ChatGPT), GPT Tutor (pedagogical guardrails with hints/verification), Control (no AI).
- Outcomes: Practice performance—GPT Base +48%, GPT Tutor +127% vs. control. Exam performance—GPT Base −17% (p<0.05), GPT Tutor no significant difference vs. control. GPT Base correct only 51%. Students did not perceive learning loss. [Bastani et al.](https://www.pnas.org/doi/10.1073/pnas.2422633122 "PNAS v122 n26, Jun 2025")
- Takeaway: Most rigorous K-12 GenAI study to date. Guardrails essential—unguarded AI harms learning. Stanford SCALE 2026 elevated this as central finding.

**Case Study 3: AI Digital Textbook Program — South Korea**
- Context: National program, 76 AI digital textbooks for math/English/informatics, launched Mar 2025. Government spent 1.2 trillion won (~$850M); publishers invested ~800B won (~$567M). Development compressed from standard 33 months to 18.
- Outcomes: Stripped of official textbook status after 4 months (Aug 2025). Adoption dropped from 37% to 19% of schools. Factual inaccuracies, technical delays, privacy risks, increased teacher workload. Teachers' union and civic groups sued MOE. [Rest of World](https://restofworld.org/2025/south-korea-ai-textbook/ "Oct 2025")
- Takeaway: Paradigmatic cautionary tale—rushed timelines, mandatory adoption without piloting, insufficient teacher/parent input, political entanglement.

**Case Study 4: Oak National Academy Aila — UK**
- Context: Government arm's-length body, £2M DfE-funded, free for all state school teachers in England. Launched Sep 2024 (beta). First 2 months: 10,000+ teachers, ~25,000 lesson plans.
- Outcomes: 85% rated quality "fairly high"/"very high"; time savings from 30 min to 5–10 min per lesson; ~2/3 reported decreased workload; >50% said Aila changed their lesson planning thinking. EEF trial: ChatGPT saved avg 25 min per planning session. [Oak National Academy](https://www.thenational.academy/blog/how-is-aila-impacting-teacher-lesson-planning-practices-workload-and-expertise-early-insights "Feb 2025")
- EEF Aila RCT (NFER, KS2, autumn 2025): results expected mid-2026—first rigorous causal evidence for purpose-built AI lesson planning. [EEF](https://educationendowmentfoundation.org.uk/projects-and-evaluation/projects/aila-teacher-choices-trial "Sep 2025")
- Takeaway: AI grounded in expert-curated curriculum outperforms generic tools; iterative step-by-step design keeps teacher as expert.

**Case Study 5: MEXT Generative AI Pilot Schools — Japan**
- Context: 52 pilot schools (4 elementary, 26 junior HS, 17 HS, 4 compulsory, 1 secondary) designated FY2023. 266 use-case reports submitted. Japanese survey (2026): 46.2% HS, 30.8% MS, 8.6% ES students use GenAI.
- Outcomes: 176 educational + 102 administrative use cases. Top educational: creating drawings/music/stories (40), refining/deepening thoughts (26). Top subjects: Foreign language (36) > Japanese (27) > Social studies (21) > Math (16). [Nishimoto et al.](https://www.uchida.co.jp/ueric/pdf/poster_generativeai.pdf "Uchidayoko Institute"); [NHK World](https://www3.nhk.or.jp/nhkworld/en/news/backstories/4680/ "Mar 2026"); [Japan Times](https://www.japantimes.co.jp/news/2026/02/26/japan/students-ai-internet/ "Feb 2026")
- Takeaway: Cautious, documentation-first approach; creative/language applications dominate early adoption.

**Case Study 6: SLS Adaptive Learning System — Singapore**
- Context: National SLS platform, all schools. AI tools: ALS (P5–S2 Math, Upper Sec Geography), Authoring Copilot (lesson creation), ShortAnsFA (marking assistance), Data Assistant (analytics), LEA (guiding questions), Appraiser Testimonial Generator (4,000+ teachers, 40,000+ testimonials).
- All AI feedback must be teacher-vetted (human-in-the-loop mandate). [GovTech](https://www.tech.gov.sg/technews/ai-in-education-transforming-singapore-education-system-with-student-learning-space/ "GovTech AI in Education"); [Singapore MOE](https://www.moe.gov.sg/education-in-sg/educational-technology-journey/edtech-masterplan/artificial-intelligence-in-education "Jan 2026")
- Takeaway: Most comprehensive government-built AI education platform globally; quantitative student outcome data not yet public.

**Case Study 7: 184 AI Education Base Schools & Squirrel AI — China**
- Context: 184 AI education base schools (Jan 2024); AI education mandatory from Sep 2025; MOE digital pilot in 10 provinces, 95 districts, 252 rural schools, 60,000+ students. Squirrel AI: 24M+ students, TIME Best Inventions 2025. iFlytek Bengbu contract: 875 schools, 400K students, ~¥800/student/year.
- Challenges: Unfunded mandates (local govts bear 85% of spending); rural 8.5M computer shortfall; iFlytek cost ¥1.7M/school; 63% of students experienced spam from data leaks; AI surveillance of facial expressions promoted; wealthy-urban vs. poor-rural divide widening.
- No rigorous English-language outcome evaluations published for 184 pilot schools. [ChinaTalk](https://www.chinatalk.media/p/chinas-ai-education-experiment "2025"); [TIME](https://time.com/collections/best-inventions-2025/7318298/squirrel-ai-intelligent-adaptive-learning-system/ "Oct 2025"); [China Daily](https://global.chinadaily.com.cn/a/202402/23/WS65d85a3aa31082fc043b8ccb.html "Feb 2024")
- Takeaway: World's largest AI-in-education initiative by scale; driven by political incentives rather than evidence; severe equity and privacy concerns.

**Case Study 8: Midjourney Art Workshop — Finland**
- Context: 10 middle school students, autumn 2022, 3 × 3-hour workshops. Published: Vartiainen et al. (2023), *IJETA*, v19(3), pp. 405–423.
- Outcomes: Qualitative socio-material insights on human-AI co-creation; prompt engineering as creative practice. [Vartiainen et al.](https://intellectdiscover.com/content/journals/10.1386/eta_00143_1 "IJETA v19 n3, 2023")
- Takeaway: One of earliest K-12 text-to-image implementations; demonstrates creative engagement; small sample limits generalizability.

**Case Study 9: Experience AI — AI Literacy — Kenya & Global**
- Context: Raspberry Pi Foundation + Google DeepMind, ages 11–14. Kenya: 441 teachers trained in 5 counties. Globally: 30,000 educators, est. 2.9M young people in 180+ countries. Won UNESCO 2025 Prize. New $4.6M expansion to 9 Latin American countries (1.25M students by 2028).
- Qualitative outcomes: teachers gained confidence; "demystified AI." No quantitative outcome data. [Raspberry Pi Foundation](https://www.raspberrypi.org/blog/impact-of-experience-ai-in-kenya/ "May 2025"); [Raspberry Pi Foundation](https://www.raspberrypi.org/blog/bringing-ai-education-to-1-25-million-students-across-latin-america/ "Mar 2026")
- Takeaway: Demonstrates scalable AI literacy education in LMICs via train-the-trainer model; one of few documented K-12 AI implementations in Africa.

**Case Study 10: Takehaya "AI Classmate" Saya — Japan**
- Context: Takehaya Elementary (Tokyo Gakugei University), 4th grade, 2025–2026. Custom AI character "Saya" positioned as a "classmate" playing devil's advocate in ethics lessons.
- Outcomes: Students valued contrarian AI perspectives; found it easier to accept challenges from AI peer than teacher. [NHK World](https://www3.nhk.or.jp/nhkworld/en/news/backstories/4680/ "Mar 2026")
- Takeaway: Novel "AI as peer debater" paradigm; supports critical thinking and perspective-taking.

**Case Study 11: EEF ChatGPT/Aila RCT — UK**
- Context: EEF-commissioned rapid trial of ChatGPT for KS3 Science lesson planning + larger NFER RCT on Aila for KS2 (autumn 2025, results mid-2026).
- Outcomes (ChatGPT trial): avg 25 min saved per planning session; caveat that teacher support needed for quality. [EEF](https://educationendowmentfoundation.org.uk/projects-and-evaluation/projects/aila-teacher-choices-trial "Sep 2025")
- Takeaway: UK is most advanced country in commissioning rigorous AI-in-education RCTs.

**Cross-Case Comparative Analysis**
- Success factors: (1) Pedagogical guardrails essential (Bastani RCT: unguarded AI −17%); (2) Teacher mediation/training investment (Newark, Aila, Singapore, Japan, Kenya vs. South Korea failure); (3) Gradual piloted scaling (Newark 1→14→66; Japan 52 pilots; Singapore progressive SLS expansion) vs. rushed rollout (South Korea); (4) Purpose-built tools > generic chatbots (Aila 85% quality, GPT Tutor +127%, vs. GPT Base 51% accuracy); (5) Dosage and consistent use (30+ min/week for Khan Academy gains); (6) Human-in-the-loop design (Singapore mandatory teacher vetting).
- Failure modes: (1) Rushed, politically driven implementation (South Korea $850M failure); (2) Inadequate piloting/evaluation (China 184 schools—no published outcomes; no U.S. K-12 causal studies); (3) Student over-reliance/shallow engagement (GPT Base crutch, "Bro IDK," immediate answer-seeking); (4) Privacy/data security failures (China 63% data leak spam; South Korea privacy risks); (5) Equity gap widening (China urban-rural divide; device/cost barriers); (6) Perceived vs. actual learning mismatch (Bastani: students unaware of −17% decline).
- Contextual factors: Centralized design (Singapore) vs. decentralized unfunded mandates (China); evidence-based evaluation culture (UK EEF) vs. political showcase projects (South Korea, China); resource levels determine implementation reality; cultural factors shape adoption speed (Japan caution, South Korea competition).

### 关键发现

**A. Academic Integrity, Over-Reliance, and Assessment Validity**
- College Board (Oct 2025, >10,000 HS students): 84% of U.S. HS students use GenAI for schoolwork; seniors and high-achievers ~2× more likely than 9th graders. [College Board](https://newsroom.collegeboard.org/new-research-majority-high-school-students-use-generative-ai-schoolwork "Oct 2025")
- RAND American Youth Panel (Dec 2025, n=1,214): AI homework use surged from 48% (May 2025) to 62% (Dec 2025). Only 9% of middle schoolers and 7% of HS students say schools permit AI for homework. 67% of students endorse statement that AI harms critical thinking (up 10pp in 10 months) yet continue using it. [RAND](https://www.rand.org/pubs/research_reports/RRA4742-1.html "RAND RR-A4742-1, Mar 2026")
- Cheating rates remain stable post-ChatGPT (peer-reviewed Lancaster et al. 2024, three U.S. high schools): self-reported cheating behaviors relatively stable; most students reject producing entire assignments via chatbot but support using AI to start assignments/explain concepts. [Lancaster et al.](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S2666920X24000560 "Computers & Education: AI, v7, Dec 2024")
- AI detection tools are unreliable: 40%+ of 6th–12th grade teachers use AI detection tools (CDT 2025); Turnitin acknowledges scores "may not always be accurate"; researcher Perkins calls them "not fit for purpose." Broward County FL spends $550K on 3-year Turnitin contract. [NPR](https://www.npr.org/2025/12/16/nx-s1-5492397/ai-schools-teachers-students "Dec 2025"); [CDT](https://cdt.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/10/FINAL-CDT-2025-Hand-in-Hand-Polling-100225-accessible.pdf "Oct 2025")
- Documented false accusations: 17-year-old at Eleanor Roosevelt HS (MD) flagged at 30.76% AI probability, grade docked, appeal ignored; non-native Mandarin speaker at Shaker Heights HS (OH) flagged due to vocabulary patterns. Stanford HAI 2023 study: AI detectors biased against non-native English writers. Students now preemptively self-check, adding ~30 min per assignment. [NPR](https://www.npr.org/2025/12/16/nx-s1-5492397/ai-schools-teachers-students "Dec 2025")
- Consequences: CDT 2025: 35% of teachers reported students not receiving credit when proven to have used AI; 17% reported accusations that could not be proven or were later shown false; 13% reported detention/suspension. [CDT](https://cdt.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/10/FINAL-CDT-2025-Hand-in-Hand-Polling-100225-accessible.pdf "Oct 2025")
- AI-resilient assessment strategies emerging: oral exams, portfolio-based assessment, in-class supervised writing, process-focused assignments with drafts/revision history. Trend from "AI-proof" → "authentic assessments" that work alongside AI. [Assessment & Evaluation in HE](https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/02602938.2026.2644516 "2026")

**B. Data Privacy, Content Safety, and Child Protection**
- Updated COPPA Rule (effective Jun 23, 2025): companies cannot retain children's data indefinitely for algorithm improvement; separate parental consent required for sharing data with third parties for AI training. Gap: internal AI training use unclear. COPPA only covers <13, leaving teens unprotected. [PIPC](https://publicinterestprivacy.org/coppa-rule-training-algorithms/ "Dec 2025")
- OpenAI (Sep 2025): new restrictions for suspected-under-18 users (no "flirtatious talk," suicide discussion guardrails, parent-settable blackout hours). Building age verification system; defaults to stricter rules in ambiguous cases. [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2025/09/16/openai-will-apply-new-restrictions-to-chatgpt-users-under-18/ "Sep 2025")
- Documented harms: wrongful death lawsuit (Adam Raine suicide after ChatGPT interactions); Character.AI lawsuit; Senate hearing "Examining the Harm of AI Chatbots" (Sep 2025); Reuters investigation of Meta chatbot policies encouraging sexual conversations with minors. [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2025/09/16/openai-will-apply-new-restrictions-to-chatgpt-users-under-18/ "Sep 2025")
- CDT 2025: 42% of students use AI chatbots as "friend/companion," 42% to "escape real life," 42% for "mental health support," 19% for "romantic relationship." 31% use school-provided devices for personal AI. 56% of conversational AI users do so weekly+. [CDT](https://cdt.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/10/FINAL-CDT-2025-Hand-in-Hand-Polling-100225-accessible.pdf "Oct 2025")
- Data breach risk: CDT found 23% of teachers reported large-scale school data breach in 2024–25; heavy AI-using teachers 28% vs. low-use 18%. [CDT](https://cdt.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/10/FINAL-CDT-2025-Hand-in-Hand-Polling-100225-accessible.pdf "Oct 2025")
- Deepfake crisis: 36% of students and 27% of teachers heard of deepfakes depicting someone at their school; 12% of students reported deepfake NCII at school; only 21% of teachers said school has deepfake NCII policy. [CDT](https://cdt.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/10/FINAL-CDT-2025-Hand-in-Hand-Polling-100225-accessible.pdf "Oct 2025")

**C. Equity, Access, and Digital Divide**
- College Board (Dec 2025): private schools 2×+ more likely than public to both permit GenAI and have policies; low-SES public schools least likely to have AI policies; rural/town schools less likely to have AI on network. "A new form of the digital divide — not simply about access to devices or the internet, but access to emerging technologies and the guidance to use them well." [College Board](https://newsroom.collegeboard.org/follow-report-ai-high-schools-genai-use-and-policy-varies-academic-achievement-attainment-grade-and "Dec 2025")
- CDT 2025: 81% of high-income (>$100K) vs. 58% of low-income (<$50K) parents report child AI use; urban 79% vs. rural 66%. [CDT](https://cdt.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/10/FINAL-CDT-2025-Hand-in-Hand-Polling-100225-accessible.pdf "Oct 2025")
- Racial patterns complex: College Board found Black and Hispanic HS students are the most frequent GenAI users (65%) vs. White (55%), Asian (58%). Key equity issue is not access alone but access to guidance and effective use support. [College Board](https://newsroom.collegeboard.org/follow-report-ai-high-schools-genai-use-and-policy-varies-academic-achievement-attainment-grade-and "Dec 2025")
- Brookings "third digital divide": rich have technology + people (teachers, parents); poor have technology alone. AI tutoring without teacher mediation may harm rather than help. [Brookings](https://www.brookings.edu/articles/ai-and-the-next-digital-divide-in-education/ "Jul 2023")
- Algorithmic bias documented: Common Sense Media (Aug 2025): AI teacher tools (MagicSchool, Khanmigo, Curipod, Gemini for Education) generated more-punitive behavior recommendations for Black-coded names vs. White-coded names. Google turned off "Generate behavior intervention strategies" shortcut after reporting. [Chalkbeat](https://www.chalkbeat.org/2025/08/06/ai-teacher-assistants-promote-racial-bias-study-finds/ "Aug 2025")
- Students with disabilities: CDT 2025: IEP/504 students use AI more (73% vs. 63% for conversations), but 57% worry AI will treat them unfairly (vs. 25%); IEP writing with AI rose from 8% to 21% in one year; 60% privacy concerns (vs. 42%). [CDT](https://cdt.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/10/FINAL-CDT-2025-Hand-in-Hand-Polling-100225-accessible.pdf "Oct 2025")

**D. Teacher Professional Identity and Workforce Implications**
- OECD TALIS 2024: 52% of teachers believe AI can enable adaptation to student abilities; but 72% believe AI enables work misrepresentation, 66% believe AI makes inappropriate recommendations. Ambivalence defines global teacher attitudes. [Education International/OECD](https://www.ei-ie.org/en/item/31815:using-artificial-intelligence-takeaways-from-talis-2024 "Dec 2025")
- CDT 2025: 50% of parents and 49% of students agree "a teacher who uses AI in class is not really doing their job." Heavy AI-using teachers more positive (80% say AI gives more time for students; 82% say enables personalized learning) vs. low-use teachers (38% find AI "more time consuming than helpful"). [CDT](https://cdt.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/10/FINAL-CDT-2025-Hand-in-Hand-Polling-100225-accessible.pdf "Oct 2025")
