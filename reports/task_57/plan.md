# Section 1：章节研究计划

## Chapter 1：The AI Investment Landscape — Capital Flows, M&A, and Strategic Partnerships
### 研究目标
- How much are major consulting firms collectively and individually spending on AI capabilities (R&D budgets, disclosed capex, venture investments) during April 2025 – April 2026?
- What are the most significant M&A transactions (acquisitions, acqui-hires) and strategic partnerships (cloud hyperscalers, AI labs, data providers) that reshape the competitive map?
- How do investment patterns differ across firm archetypes — Big Four, MBB, and technology consultancies?

### 关键发现

**A. Disclosed AI Investment Commitments**

- 发现 1: Deloitte allocated over $3 billion in GenAI investments through FY2030, announced alongside FY2025 results (global revenue $70.5 billion, +4.8% in local currency). [Deloitte Global Revenue Announcement](https://www.deloitte.com/global/en/about/press-room/global-revenue-announcement.html "Deloitte FY2025 revenue press release, September 2025")
- 发现 2: PwC invested nearly $1.5 billion to expand and scale AI capabilities across its global network during FY2025, up from the original $1 billion over three years announced in April 2023. PwC reported record global revenues of $56.9 billion (12 months ending June 2025). [PwC Global Annual Review 2025](https://www.pwc.com/gx/en/about/global-annual-review.html "PwC GAR FY2025, October 2025")
- 发现 3: EY invests more than $1 billion annually to develop AI-first platforms and products, including 1,000 AI agents and 100+ internal AI applications. AI-related revenue grew 30% YoY in FY2025 (total global revenue $53.2 billion). EY also made a $1 billion Assurance technology investment incorporating AI. [EY FY2025 Revenue Announcement](https://www.ey.com/en_gl/newsroom/2025/10/ey-announces-global-revenue-of-us-53-2b-for-fiscal-year-2025 "EY global revenue press release, October 2025")
- 发现 4: KPMG committed $2 billion over five years (July 2023) to expand AI and cloud services via Microsoft, plus $100 million over four years for Google Cloud AI partnership (November 2024). [Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/technology/artificial-intelligence/kpmg-spend-100-million-ai-partnership-with-google-cloud-2024-11-20/ "KPMG $100M Google Cloud AI partnership, November 2024")
- 发现 5: McKinsey does not publicly disclose total AI investment figures; its QuantumBlack AI division has scaled significantly and internal tool "Lilli" is deployed firm-wide. McKinsey was named one of four inaugural OpenAI "Frontier Alliance" partners (February 2026). [McKinsey–OpenAI Frontier Alliance](https://www.mckinsey.com/about-us/new-at-mckinsey-blog/mckinsey-and-openai-scale-ai-driven-transformations-with-new-frontier-alliance "McKinsey official blog, February 2026")
- 发现 6: Bain expanded its OpenAI partnership (October 2024), establishing a dedicated OpenAI Center of Excellence (CoE) for retail and healthcare/life sciences using GPT-4o and o1 models. [Bain Press Release](https://www.bain.com/about/media-center/press-releases/2024/bain-and-company-announces-expanded-partnership-with-openai-to-accelerate-delivery-of-ai-solutions-and-meet-fast-growing-client-needs/ "Bain press release, October 2024")
- 发现 7: BCG was named an OpenAI Frontier Alliance partner (February 2026). BCG AI Radar survey (January 2025) found one in three companies globally planned to allocate >$25 million to AI in 2025, and corporations expected to double AI spending from 0.8% to ~1.7% of revenues in 2026. [BCG AI Radar](https://www.bcg.com/press/15january2025-ai-optimism-autonomous-agents "BCG press release, January 2025")

IBM spent $8.3 billion on R&D in FY2025 — R&D intensity of 12.3% of total revenue. CEO Arvind Krishna announced $150 billion investment in America over five years including $30 billion+ in R&D. [IBM Q4 2025 Earnings](https://newsroom.ibm.com/2026-01-28-IBM-RELEASES-FOURTH-QUARTER-RESULTS?hl=en-US "IBM Q4 2025 press release, January 2026") [IBM $150B US Investment](https://newsroom.ibm.com/2025-04-28-ibm-unveils-150-billion-investment-in-america-to-accelerate-technology-opportunity "IBM press release, April 2025")

**D. Investment Pattern Comparisons**

- 发现 21: Technology consultancies invest at the largest absolute scale: Accenture ($3 billion GenAI commitment, $2.7 billion AI revenue), IBM ($8.3 billion R&D, $12.5 billion GenAI book of business), Capgemini ($3.3 billion WNS acquisition alone). Accenture's AI workforce nearly doubled from 40,000 to 77,000 in two years.

[IBM Q4 2025 Earnings](https://newsroom.ibm.com/2026-01-28-IBM-RELEASES-FOURTH-QUARTER-RESULTS?hl=en-US "IBM Q4 2025 press release, January 2026")

# Section 2：给 Write 阶段的执行建议

1. **Firm-naming consistency.** Adopt fixed short-name convention: "Deloitte," "PwC," "EY," "KPMG," "Accenture," "McKinsey," "BCG," "Bain," "IBM Consulting," "Capgemini." Spell the full legal name once on first mention in each chapter, then use the short form.

2. **Currency and financial figures.** Report all monetary values in USD. If the original source uses another currency, provide the original figure followed by approximate USD equivalent in parentheses. State whether figures are nominal or inflation-adjusted, and specify fiscal-year conventions (e.g., Accenture FY ends August 31).

3. **Temporal anchoring.** Every quantitative claim must be pinned to a specific time point or range (e.g., "FY2025," "Q3 2025," "April 2025 – March 2026"). Avoid vague references such as "recently" or "in recent years."

4. **First-party verification requirements.** The following claim types must be sourced from T1/T2 sources: (a) revenue and investment figures, (b) headcount numbers, (c) named product capabilities, (d) M&A transaction values, (e) any forward-looking guidance attributed to firm leadership.

5. **Avoiding chapter overlap.** Chapter 2 (Products) describes *what was built*; Chapter 3 (Case Studies) describes *how it was deployed for clients and with what results*. Chapter 4 (Strategy) references products and cases only at a strategic-positioning level. Chapter 5 (Talent) covers workforce programs, not general firm-strategy rhetoric.

6. **Balanced firm coverage.** Ensure no single firm dominates more than ~25% of the narrative in any chapter. Where data availability is uneven, explicitly acknowledge the asymmetry rather than letting it silently skew analysis.

7. **Distinguishing disclosed facts from analyst estimates.** Clearly label whether a figure is a firm's own disclosure, an analyst's estimate, or a media report's approximation. Use qualifying language such as "according to Accenture's FY2025 10-K" versus "as estimated by IDC."

8. **Tone and depth.** Maintain a formal analytical tone — akin to sell-side equity-research or Gartner market analysis. Avoid promotional language. Each chapter should be 2,500–4,000 words in final form.

9. **Handling confidential or unverifiable claims.** If a firm's press release makes a claim that cannot be independently verified, present it as the firm's stated claim with appropriate attribution, not as established fact.

10. **Forward-looking statements in Chapter 7.** All outlook assertions must be grounded in at least one of: (a) disclosed firm guidance, (b) a credible third-party forecast with named institution and date, or (c) a logical extrapolation from documented trends in earlier chapters.
