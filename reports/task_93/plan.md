# Section 2: Writing Guidelines for Write Phase

## Tone and Style
- Formal, analytical market-research register throughout. The report should read like a professional research publication from a credible industry-analysis firm.
- Active voice preferred for clarity; passive voice acceptable for established conventions (e.g., "the market was valued at…").
- Avoid superlatives ("best," "revolutionary," "game-changing") unless directly quoting a source. Prefer measured, evidence-anchored language ("significant," "substantial," "notable growth").
- Maintain an objective, balanced stance when comparing vendors.
- Use "we" sparingly and only for analytical judgments ("we estimate," "we assess that").

## Cross-Chapter Consistency
- **Currency**: All monetary figures in USD. If a source reports in another currency, convert at the applicable exchange rate and note the original figure.
- **Time references**: Analytical present is April 2026. Use "as of early 2026" or "during the April 2025–April 2026 research window" for current-state claims. Past tense for completed events. Future tense or conditional for projections beyond 2026.
- **Market-size figures**: Explicitly acknowledge divergence across research firms in Chapter 1 and adopt a clearly stated working definition. Subsequent chapters should reference the adopted definition and cite ranges rather than single definitive numbers.
- **Product naming**: Full official product names on first mention with vendor in parentheses (e.g., "DaVinci Resolve (Blackmagic Design)"), shortened forms thereafter. Maintain consistent short names across all chapters.
- **Data vintage**: Always state the date/year of any market-size or user-count figure.

## Standardized Key Definitions
- **Video editing software**: Applications for post-production manipulation of recorded video (cutting, sequencing, color correction, audio mixing, titling, effects). Includes desktop NLEs, mobile editing apps, and cloud/browser-based editors.
- **Video creation tools**: Broader category encompassing video editing software plus AI-powered video generation tools (text-to-video, image-to-video), motion graphics authoring, and template-driven video assembly platforms.
- **AI-native editing tools**: Products built with AI/ML at their core (e.g., Descript, Runway ML), distinct from traditional NLEs with incremental AI feature additions.
- **Freemium model**: Functional free tier with monetization through premium upgrades, watermark removal, or expanded storage/export quality.
- **NLE (Non-Linear Editor)**: Timeline-based video editing software; expand on first mention, abbreviate thereafter.
- **Creator economy**: Ecosystem of independent content creators producing and monetizing digital content — a key demand driver for prosumer editing tools.

## Risks and Caveats for the Writer
- **Market-size estimate divergence**: Published estimates vary by 3–5× depending on scope. Must acknowledge and explain variation.
- **CapCut/ByteDance data opacity**: No public CapCut-specific revenue disclosures. Third-party user-base estimates should be clearly labeled as estimates.
- **AI-tool boundary ambiguity**: Line between "video editing" and "AI video generation" is actively blurring. Report must state where it draws the definitional line.
- **Geopolitical sensitivity**: Present factual regulatory status for ByteDance without speculating on ultimate outcomes; note the date of most recent regulatory developments.
- **Rapid product-cycle risk**: Timestamp all product-specific claims; note that pricing/features may change.
- **Segment data scarcity**: Professional/prosumer/consumer revenue breakdowns are rarely published with high confidence. Clearly indicate where segment-level sizing is analyst-estimated.
- **Free-tier economics**: Products like DaVinci Resolve (free), CapCut (free), and Clipchamp generate large user bases but minimal direct revenue — revenue-based share can be misleading.

## Research Time Window
- Primary research window: April 2025 – April 2026 (12 months to current date 2026-04-08).
- Historical context may extend earlier where needed for trend analysis.
- Forward projections use conditional language and cite source forecasts through 2030–2032.
