# Section 1：章节研究计划

## Chapter 1：Problem Formulation and Historical Context
### 研究目标
- Define the formal model of the two-bidder FPSB auction with independent private values drawn from heterogeneous distributions (ex-ante asymmetric bidders).
- Introduce notation: valuation distributions $F_1, F_2$ on supports $[\underline{v}_i, \bar{v}_i]$, bidding strategy $\beta_i(v_i)$, interim expected payoff, and BNE concept.
- Derive the first-order-condition / ODE system that characterizes equilibrium.
- Trace the intellectual-history timeline from Vickrey (1961) through Maskin–Riley, Plum, Lebrun, Kaplan–Zamir.
- Explain why asymmetry fundamentally changes tractability compared with the symmetric case.

### 关键发现
- 发现 1: The standard two-bidder FPSB model has bidders $i \in \{1,2\}$ with $v_i \sim F_i$ (continuous positive density $f_i$ on $[\underline{v}_i, \bar{v}_i]$) maximizing $\Pi_i(b, v_i) = F_j(\beta_j^{-1}(b))(v_i - b)$. The formulation is explicitly stated in [Maskin & Riley (2000)](https://www.isid.ac.in/~dmishra/topicsdoc/maskin_riley.pdf "Asymmetric Auctions, RES 67(3): 413–438"), equations (2.2)–(2.3).
- 发现 2: The **direct-form** coupled ODE system is $\beta_i'(v_i) = \frac{f_j(\beta_j^{-1}(\beta_i(v_i)))}{F_j(\beta_j^{-1}(\beta_i(v_i)))} \cdot \frac{(v_i - \beta_i(v_i))}{\beta_j'(\beta_j^{-1}(\beta_i(v_i)))}$. [Plum (1992)](https://kylewoodward.com/blog-data/pdfs/references/plum-international-journal-of-game-theory-1992A.pdf "Int. J. Game Theory 20(4): 393–418") showed equilibrium strategies are $C^1$, strictly increasing, and "completely characterized by a BVP for a system of singular differential equations."
- 发现 3: The **inverse-bid formulation** defines $\phi_i(b) = \beta_i^{-1}(b)$ and yields the cleaner system $\phi_i'(b) = \frac{F_j(\phi_j(b))}{f_j(\phi_j(b))} \cdot \frac{1}{\phi_i(b) - b}$, equivalently $\frac{d}{db}\ln F_j(\phi_j(b)) = \frac{1}{\phi_i(b) - b}$. This is the standard form used in [Maskin & Riley (2000)](https://www.isid.ac.in/~dmishra/topicsdoc/maskin_riley.pdf "RES 67(3): 413–438") eq. (3.12) and [Lebrun (1999)](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/abs/10.1111/1468-2354.00008 "IER 40(1): 125–142").
- 发现 4: Three boundary conditions: (B1) common minimum bid $\beta_1(\underline{v}_1) = \beta_2(\underline{v}_2) = \underline{b}$; (B2) $\phi_i(\underline{b}) = \underline{v}_i$; (B3) common upper endpoint $\bar{b} = \beta_1(\bar{v}_1) = \beta_2(\bar{v}_2)$, established by [Lebrun (1999)](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/abs/10.1111/1468-2354.00008 "IER 40(1): 125–142") and [Maskin & Riley (2000)](https://www.isid.ac.in/~dmishra/topicsdoc/maskin_riley.pdf "RES 67(3): 413–438").
- 发现 5: In the symmetric case $F_1 = F_2 = F$, the system decouples to a single ODE with closed-form solution $\beta(v) = v - \int_0^v \frac{F(y)}{F(v)}dy = E[Y_1 | Y_1 < v]$. For $F = U[0,1]$ and two bidders, $\beta(v) = v/2$. First derived by [Vickrey (1961)](https://cramton.umd.edu/market-design-papers/vickrey-counterspeculation-auctions-and-competitive-sealed-tenders.pdf "J. Finance 16(1): 8–37"); textbook exposition in [Krishna (2010)](https://bugarinmauricio.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/06/krishna-auction-theory-caps1a4.pdf "Auction Theory, 2nd Ed., Academic Press") Proposition 2.2.
- 发现 6: Asymmetry destroys tractability because when $F_1 \neq F_2$, no symmetric equilibrium exists; the coupled ODE for $\beta_1'$ depends on $\beta_2$ and vice versa, making it an irreducibly coupled BVP for two singular ODEs with a free upper boundary. Vickrey (1961, Appendix II) first identified this, noting the ODE "resists solution by analytical methods." [Kaplan & Zamir (2012)](http://www.ma.huji.ac.il/~zamir/documents/Uniform_fulltext.pdf "Economic Theory 50(2): 269–302") confirmed this was an open problem for over 40 years.
- 发现 7: **Historical timeline**: Vickrey (1961) → Griesmer–Levitan–Shubik (1967, solved uniform-on-different-supports with common lower bound) → Maskin & Riley (circulated 1980s as UCLA WP #254, published 2000, comprehensive theory) → Plum (1992, characterization via singular BVP, solved power-function family) → Lebrun (1996 existence; 1999 $n$-bidder characterization + uniqueness subcases; 2006 general uniqueness under log-concavity) → Kaplan & Zamir (2012, complete analytical solution for general uniform case).
- 发现 8: The ODE system is singular at $b = \underline{b}$: both $F_i(\phi_i(b))$ and $(\phi_i(b) - b)$ vanish, producing a $0/0$ indeterminacy. L'Hôpital's rule gives $\phi_i'(\underline{b}) = 2$ in the common-lower-bound case. [Plum (1992)](https://kylewoodward.com/blog-data/pdfs/references/plum-international-journal-of-game-theory-1992A.pdf "Int. J. Game Theory 20(4): 393–418") eq. (A.4); [Lebrun (2006)](https://econ.laps.yorku.ca/files/2015/10/lebrunb-u.pdf "GEB 55(1): 131–151") notes this singularity prevents standard ODE uniqueness theorems from applying directly.
- 发现 9: Maskin & Riley (2000) define **conditional stochastic dominance (CSD)** as the key structural assumption for their revenue comparisons. Under CSD, the strong buyer's bid distribution stochastically dominates the weak buyer's, and $\phi_s(b) > \phi_w(b)$ for interior bids (Proposition 3.5).

## Chapter 2：Existence, Uniqueness, and Structural Properties of Equilibrium
### 研究目标
- Survey theoretical results on existence and uniqueness of BNE in monotone pure strategies for the asymmetric two-bidder FPSB auction.
- Present key conditions on distributions (continuity, positive density, bounded support) under which existence/uniqueness hold.
- Reference contributions of Lebrun (1999, 2006), Maskin & Riley (2000, 2003), Lizzeri & Persico (2000).
- Discuss structural properties: strict monotonicity, Lipschitz continuity, common upper endpoint of bid range, boundary conditions.
- Address the subtlety that the weaker bidder's strategy may not span the full bid range.

### 关键发现
- 发现 1: **Existence (Lebrun 1996)** — first general existence proof for $n$ bidders IPV, introducing efficient tie-breaking when the standard fair rule fails. Required only continuous $F_i$ on interval supports; no positive-density assumption needed for existence alone. [Lebrun (2006)](https://econ.laps.yorku.ca/files/2015/10/lebrunb-u.pdf "GEB 55(1): 131–151, Section 1") cites this as the starting point.
- 发现 2: **Existence (Maskin & Riley 2000b, RES 67: 439–454)** — proved via discontinuous-game theory (Dasgupta–Maskin, Reny). Conditions: $n$ risk-neutral or risk-averse bidders, $F_i$ twice continuously differentiable with $f_i > 0$, and $F_i(\underline{s}_i) > 0$ (mass point at lower endpoint, automatically satisfied with a reserve price). [Maskin & Riley (2003)](https://www.ias.edu/sites/default/files/sss/papers/econpaper31.pdf "GEB 45(2): 395–409, Section 4").
- 发现 3: **Existence (Athey 2001, Econometrica 69(4): 861–889)** — broadest existence result via single-crossing condition (SCC) and Kakutani fixed-point theorem. Covers affiliated values, heterogeneous bidders, different supports, bidder-specific reserve prices. For continuum-action games, takes limits of finite-action PSNE via Helly's Selection Theorem. [Athey (2001)](https://kylewoodward.com/blog-data/pdfs/references/athey-econometrica-2001A.pdf "Econometrica 69(4): 861–889, Section 4, Theorem 7").
- 发现 4: **Uniqueness (Maskin & Riley 2003, GEB 45(2): 395–409)** — for $n=2$: requires IPV, $F_i$ twice $C^2$ with $f_i > 0$, and $F_i(\underline{s}_i) > 0$. Proof applies FTODE at the common upper endpoint $\bar{b}$; key Lemma 9 establishes a monotone comparison principle on log-CDF solutions. For $n$ bidders: additionally requires common upper support $\bar{s}_1 = \cdots = \bar{s}_n$, identical preferences at same reservation price, and NIARA. [Maskin & Riley (2003)](https://www.ias.edu/sites/default/files/sss/papers/econpaper31.pdf "GEB 45(2): 395–409, Propositions 1–2").
- 发现 5: **Uniqueness (Lebrun 2006, GEB 55(1): 131–151)** — Theorem 1: uniqueness under the weakest known conditions. For atomless distributions ($F_i(c_i) = 0$) with common or different supports, requires only **local log-concavity** of $F_i$ at the highest lower support endpoint $c_1$. The "sliding" argument: translate one solution along the 45° line by $\varepsilon$; log-concavity implies the translated function satisfies strict differential inequalities, preventing two distinct solutions from both meeting the lower boundary condition. More general than Maskin & Riley (2003): no mass-point requirement, applies to atomless case with different supports. [Lebrun (2006)](https://econ.laps.yorku.ca/files/2015/10/lebrunb-u.pdf "GEB 55(1): 131–151, Theorem 1 and Section 4").
- 发现 6: **Uniqueness (Lizzeri & Persico 2000, GEB 30(1): 83–114)** — proved both existence and uniqueness for two-player bidding games with affiliated (correlated) values. Extends beyond IPV to interdependent values for $n=2$; requires symmetry for $n > 2$. Cited in [Athey (2001)](https://kylewoodward.com/blog-data/pdfs/references/athey-econometrica-2001A.pdf "Econometrica 69(4): 861–889, footnote 6") and [Maskin & Riley (2003)](https://www.ias.edu/sites/default/files/sss/papers/econpaper31.pdf "GEB 45(2): 395–409, Section 4").
- 发现 7: **Structural properties** — (a) Strict monotonicity: Plum (1992) for $n=2$, Lebrun (1999) for $n$ bidders. (b) $C^1$ regularity: Plum (1992). (c) Common upper endpoint $\bar{b}$: Lebrun (1999) for common supports, Maskin & Riley (2003) Lemma 10 for equal upper supports, Lebrun (2006) C.5 for general supports. (d) No gaps in bid support: Maskin & Riley (2003) Lemma 11 for $n$ bidders under NIARA; Lemma 1 suffices for $n=2$.
- 发现 8: **Weaker bidder drops out early** — when $d_2 < d_1$, bidder 2's bid function satisfies $\beta_2(d_2) = \eta_2 < \bar{b}$, so above $\eta_2$ only the stronger bidder is active. Formalized in [Lebrun (2006)](https://econ.laps.yorku.ca/files/2015/10/lebrunb-u.pdf "GEB 55(1): 131–151, Section 5.2, Characterization C.5").
- 发现 9: **Log-concavity is needed only locally** — at $c_1$, the highest lower support endpoint. Most standard distributions (uniform, normal, logistic, exponential, power-function) satisfy this globally (Bagnoli & Bergstrom 1989). Also applicable to cartel settings where the cartel CDF is $F^m$ (log-concavity of $F^m$ follows from log-concavity of $F$). [Lebrun (2006)](https://econ.laps.yorku.ca/files/2015/10/lebrunb-u.pdf "GEB 55(1): 131–151, Section 2").
- 发现 10: **Proof technique comparison** — ODE-based (Lebrun/Plum): work from terminal condition where ODE is nonsingular, characterize existence/uniqueness of $\eta$ satisfying the initial condition; handles atomless case. FTODE (Maskin & Riley 2003): transform to log-CDF $p_i(b) = \ln F_i(\phi_i(b))$, apply FTODE; requires mass point to eliminate singularity. Athey (2001): no ODE at all; uses SCC + Kakutani; broadest scope but does not yield uniqueness or differentiability.

## Chapter 3：Closed-Form and Analytical Solutions
### 研究目标
- Catalog known cases admitting closed-form equilibrium strategies: (a) uniform distributions on different supports, (b) power-function/Pareto-type distributions, (c) certain exponential or one-parameter families.
- Present explicit bidding strategies and the algebraic mechanism making the ODE system tractable.
- Discuss the Kaplan–Zamir (2012) compendium and war-of-attrition / all-pay-auction mappings.
- Clarify the sharp boundary: outside these special families, no general closed-form solution exists.

### 关键发现
- 发现 1: **Uniform, common lower bound — GLS (1967).** For $v_1 \sim U[0, \bar{v}_1]$, $v_2 \sim U[0, \bar{v}_2]$, the ODE simplifies to $\phi_i'(b)(\phi_j(b) - b) = \phi_i(b)$ (polynomial), solvable by algebraic substitution. Common upper bid $\bar{b} = \bar{v}_1\bar{v}_2/(\bar{v}_1 + \bar{v}_2)$. Special case of [Plum (1992)](https://kylewoodward.com/blog-data/pdfs/references/plum-international-journal-of-game-theory-1992A.pdf "Int. J. Game Theory 20(4): 393–418") with $\mu = 0$, $\alpha = 0$. Cited in [Kaplan & Zamir (2012)](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s00199-010-0563-9 "Economic Theory 50(2): 269–302").
- 发现 2: **General uniform — Kaplan & Zamir (2012).** Complete solution for $v_i \sim U[\underline{v}_i, \bar{v}_i]$ including binding minimum bid $m$. Three regimes: (R1) $m \leq (\underline{v}_1+\underline{v}_2)/2$ → exponential form (Proposition 1); (R2) $m > (\underline{v}_1+\underline{v}_2)/2$, $m \neq \underline{v}_2$ → power-function form (Proposition 2); (R3) $m = \underline{v}_2$ → logarithmic form (Proposition 3). This completed the problem Vickrey (1961) declared intractable. [Kaplan & Zamir (2012)](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s00199-010-0563-9 "Economic Theory 50(2): 269–302").
- 发现 3: **Algebraic mechanism for uniform tractability.** The key property is $F_i(\phi_i)/f_i(\phi_i) = \phi_i - \underline{v}_i$ (linear in $\phi_i$), transforming the coupled ODE into a polynomial system amenable to "integration by differentiation." [Plum (1992)](https://kylewoodward.com/blog-data/pdfs/references/plum-international-journal-of-game-theory-1992A.pdf "Int. J. Game Theory 20(4): 393–418, Section 4").
- 发现 4: **Power-function family — Plum (1992).** For $\varphi_i(x) = c_i(x-\alpha)^\mu$ on $(\alpha, \beta_i)$ with common $\mu$ but different upper supports, the ODE system becomes polynomial ($F_i/f_i = (v-\alpha)/(\mu+1)$, linear). Plum's Theorem 3 gives explicit bid functions involving power expressions. Solved via logarithmic differentiation yielding a separable ODE. [Plum (1992)](https://kylewoodward.com/blog-data/pdfs/references/plum-international-journal-of-game-theory-1992A.pdf "Int. J. Game Theory 20(4): 393–418, Theorem 3, pp. 401–402").
- 发现 5: **Power distributions — Cheng (2006).** For $F_i(v) = v^{\alpha_i}$ on $[0,1]$ with different exponents, equilibrium strategies are linear: $\beta_i(v) = \frac{\alpha_j}{\alpha_i + \alpha_j}v$. Distinct from Plum: allows different exponents but requires common support $[0,1]$. Referenced in [Hubbard, Kirkegaard & Paarsch (2012)](https://vinci.cs.uiowa.edu/~hjp/download/hkp.pdf "Using Economic Theory to Guide Numerical Analysis, pp. 6–7").
- 发现 6: **Complete catalog of tractable families.** Six known categories: (1) symmetric $F_1=F_2$ (any), Vickrey 1961; (2) $U[0,\bar{v}_i]$, GLS 1967; (3) $c_i(v-\alpha)^\mu$, Plum 1992; (4) $v^{\alpha_i}$ on $[0,1]$, Cheng 2006; (5) general $U[\underline{v}_i,\bar{v}_i]$, Kaplan–Zamir 2012; (6) general uniform + minimum bid, Kaplan–Zamir 2012. **No other distributional families** are known to admit closed-form solutions.
- 发现 7: **Tractability condition.** All tractable families share $F_i/f_i$ being linear in the valuation variable. When $F_i/f_i$ is nonlinear (exponential, normal, etc.), the coupled ODE becomes analytically intractable. No closed-form solution is known for asymmetric exponential distributions despite having closed-form symmetric solutions.
- 发现 8: **"No general closed form" is consensus, not a theorem.** [Hubbard et al. (2012)](https://vinci.cs.uiowa.edu/~hjp/download/hkp.pdf "Using Economic Theory to Guide Numerical Analysis, p. 1") state: "Typically, it is impossible to derive equilibrium bid functions explicitly in asymmetric first-price auction models." [Fibich & Gavish (2011)](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0899825611000509 "GEB 73(2): 479–495") state: "no closed form solutions for asymmetric auctions — except for uniform distributions." No formal impossibility proof (e.g., via differential Galois theory) exists; it is an empirical observation about the ODE structure.
- 发现 9: **No useful all-pay / war-of-attrition mapping under asymmetry.** Revenue equivalence fails when $F_1 \neq F_2$, so there is no direct mapping between the asymmetric FPSB and other auction formats that would yield alternative closed-form strategies. [Krishna (2010)](https://bugarinmauricio.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/06/krishna-auction-theory-caps1a4.pdf "Auction Theory, 2nd Ed., Proposition 3.1").

## Chapter 4：Approximate and Numerical Methods
### 研究目标
- Present perturbation/asymptotic methods, especially Fibich–Gavious (2003) small-asymmetry expansion.
- Cover numerical ODE / BVP solvers: backward-shooting, finite-difference, collocation schemes, and more recent approaches (Bajari 2001; Marshall et al. 2005; Gayle–Richard 2008).
- Discuss convergence, accuracy, computational cost, and known pitfalls (stiffness near boundary, initial-condition sensitivity).
- Note available open-source or reference implementations.

### 关键发现
- 发现 1: **Fibich–Gavious (2003) perturbation framework.** Write each $F_i = F_{\mathrm{avg}} + \varepsilon H_i$, where $\varepsilon = \max_i\max_v|F_i - F_{\mathrm{avg}}|$ is the small parameter. Zeroth-order term: symmetric equilibrium $b^{\mathrm{sym}}(v)$. First-order correction (Proposition 1, Eq. 17): $b_i(v) = b^{\mathrm{sym}}(v) + \varepsilon B_i(v; H_i) + O(\varepsilon^2)$, where $B_i$ involves an integral depending on $H_i/F$. Remarkably accurate even at $\varepsilon = 0.5$. [Fibich & Gavious (2003)](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/220442635_Asymmetric_First-Price_Auctions-A_Perturbation_Approach "Math. Oper. Res. 28(4): 836–852").
- 发现 2: **Revenue equivalence at $O(\varepsilon)$.** Under weak asymmetry, $R^{\mathrm{2nd}} - R^{\mathrm{1st}} = O(\varepsilon^2)$: first-price and second-price auctions yield the same expected revenue to first order. Generalized in Fibich, Gavious & Sela (2003, JET 115: 309–321) to all standard mechanisms.
- 发现 3: **No formal convergence radius.** The expansion is a formal first-order Taylor expansion; no convergent power series in $\varepsilon$ has been proven. Practical accuracy far exceeds formal validity.
- 发现 4: **Backward-shooting is inherently unstable (Fibich & Gavish 2011).** Error from initial perturbation $\varepsilon$ in $\bar{b}$ grows as $|\varepsilon|/F^{n-1}(v) \to \infty$ as $v \to 0$. For $n=10$ and machine precision, breakdown occurs at $v_{\min} \approx 0.033$; for $n=100$, $v_{\min} \approx 0.70$. This is an **analytic** instability, not a numerical artifact. [Fibich & Gavish (2011)](http://www.math.tau.ac.il/~fibich/Manuscripts/Numerical-simulations-of-asymmetric-first-price-auctions.pdf "GEB 73(2): 479–495, Lemmas 3.1–3.2").
- 发现 5: **Fibich–Gavish boundary-value method (2011).** Change of independent variable from $b$ to $v_n$ transforms the free-boundary BVP into a fixed-domain BVP on $[0,1]$. Solved by Newton's method (~5 iterations, quadratic convergence) or fixed-point iterations (~25–50 iterations). Discretization error scales as $h^4$ with fourth-order finite differences. Handles up to $n=450$ bidders. Compact Matlab implementations (~40 lines each) provided in the paper's appendices. [Fibich & Gavish (2011)](http://www.math.tau.ac.il/~fibich/Manuscripts/Numerical-simulations-of-asymmetric-first-price-auctions.pdf "GEB 73(2): 479–495, §4–5").
- 发现 6: **Marshall et al. (1994): Pioneering backward-shooting.** First numerical method; transformed ODE system using $\delta_i(t) = t/h_i(t)$, piecewise Taylor-series backward from $\bar{b}$. Achieved 8-digit accuracy for $\bar{b}$ but identified instability near origin. [Marshall et al. (1994)](https://capcp.la.psu.edu/wp-content/uploads/sites/11/numericalanalysis.pdf "GEB 7(2): 193–220").
- 发现 7: **Hubbard–Kirkegaard–Paarsch (2012): Chebyshev MPEC.** Approximate inverse-bid functions by Chebyshev polynomials of degree $K$; coefficients chosen by minimizing FOC residuals at Chebyshev nodes subject to boundary/tangency constraints. Key finding: low-degree ($K=3$ or $5$) approximations yield qualitatively wrong results (wrong crossing behavior, wrong revenue rankings); degree-25 needed for consistency with theory. No formal convergence proof exists for any computational approach. [HKP (2012)](https://vinci.cs.uiowa.edu/~hjp/download/hkp.pdf "Using Economic Theory to Guide Numerical Analysis").
- 发现 8: **Gayle & Richard (2008): Automated Taylor-series backward solver.** Generalized MMRS to arbitrary distributions via an algebra of local Taylor-series expansions with B-spline interpolation. FORTRAN-90 implementation handles Weibull, beta, normal, lognormal distributions and coalitions. [Gayle & Richard (2008)](https://capcp.la.psu.edu/wp-content/uploads/sites/11/2020/07/NumericalSolutions.pdf "Comp. Econ. 32(3): 245–278").
- 发现 9: **Au–Banks–Guo (2021): BID algorithm.** Constructs finite-action PSNE converging to continuum-action PSNE by computing indifference points. Distinct from ODE methods; works directly in strategy space. Convergence proven conditional on knowing $\bar{b}$. [Au et al. (2021)](https://ideas.repec.org/a/inm/ordeca/v18y2021i4p321-334.html "Decision Analysis 18(4): 321–334").
- 发现 10: **Available implementations.** BIDCOMP2 (Li–Riley–Tang, backward-shooting, power/normal only, URL possibly defunct); Fibich–Gavish Matlab codes (Appendices A–B, ~40 lines); Gayle–Richard FORTRAN-90 (from authors); HKP AMPL code (with SNOPT/KNITRO); pyCollocation Python package (GitHub, auction-model example notebook).
- 发现 11: **Dynamical-systems approach (Fibich & Gavish 2012).** Equilibrium corresponds to a saddle point; backward integration follows the unstable manifold, explaining the inherent instability. Yields boundary-layer analysis for large-$n$ auctions: strategies become symmetric except in an $O(1/n^2)$ layer near $\bar{v}$. [Fibich & Gavish (2012)](https://pubsonline.informs.org/doi/10.1287/moor.1110.0535 "Math. Oper. Res. 37(2): 219–243").

## Chapter 5：Economic Implications — Revenue, Efficiency, and Bid Shading
### 研究目标
- Revenue comparison: asymmetric FPSB vs. second-price (Vickrey) auction and symmetric FPSB benchmark; Maskin–Riley (2000) ambiguity result.
- Allocative efficiency: the weaker bidder shades less, sometimes winning against a stronger rival → general inefficiency.
- Bid-shading patterns: how each bidder's shading depends on degree and direction of asymmetry; connection to perturbation literature.
- Brief connection to optimal auction design (Myerson 1981) and reserve-price setting.

### 关键发现
- 发现 1: **Revenue ranking is ambiguous under asymmetry.** [Maskin & Riley (2000)](https://www.isid.ac.in/~dmishra/topicsdoc/maskin_riley.pdf "RES 67(3): 413–438, Section 4") show three types of asymmetry have opposing effects: **shifts** favor FPSB (Prop. 4.3) — e.g., $v_w \sim U[0,1]$, $v_s \sim U[2,3]$ gives FPSB revenue = 1 vs. SPA revenue = 1/2; **stretches** favor FPSB (Prop. 4.4); **mass reallocation to lower endpoint** favors SPA (Prop. 4.5) — the "lowballing" mechanism.
- 发现 2: **Numerical magnitudes (Maskin & Riley 2000, Section 5).** For uniform shifts: FPSB revenue advantage is 0% at $a_s=0$, 6.1% at $a_s=1/4$, 19.6% at $a_s=1$, 37.2% at $a_s=3$ (Table 5.1). For stretches: 10.1% at $\alpha_s=2$, 21.2% at $\alpha_s=3$ (Table 5.2). For mass reallocation: SPA advantage is 9.7% at $\eta_s=1$, 48.5% at $\eta_s=3$ (Table 5.3).
- 发现 3: **$O(\varepsilon^2)$ revenue equivalence under weak asymmetry.** $R^{\mathrm{1st}} = R_{\mathrm{sym}}[F_{\mathrm{avg}}] + O(\varepsilon^2)$: the symmetric revenue at the average distribution approximates asymmetric FPSB revenue with only second-order error. [Fibich & Gavious (2003)](http://www.math.tau.ac.il/~fibich/Manuscripts/first_rev_final.pdf "MOR 28(4): 836–852, Proposition 6"). Extended to $O(\varepsilon^2/n^3)$ by [Fibich, Gavious & Gavish (2018)](http://www.math.tau.ac.il/~fibich/Manuscripts/RET_large_SIAP18.pdf "SIAM J. Appl. Math. 78(3): 1489–1510, Theorem 5.1").
- 发现 4: **Asymmetry generically decreases revenue.** Corollary 4.3 of [Fibich et al. (2018)](http://www.math.tau.ac.il/~fibich/Manuscripts/RET_large_SIAP18.pdf "SIAM J. Appl. Math. 78(3): 1489–1510"): $R[F_1,\ldots,F_n] - R[F_G,\ldots,F_G] \sim -\frac{1}{n^2}\frac{\mathrm{Var}[f_1(1),\ldots,f_n(1)]}{f_G^3(1)}$. Asymmetry is revenue-reducing at $O(1/n^2)$.
- 发现 5: **FPSB is generically inefficient under asymmetry.** The weak bidder shades less → may win despite lower valuation. SPA is always efficient under IPV (truth-telling is dominant). Misallocation occurs when $v_s > v_w$ but $\beta_s(v_s) < \beta_w(v_w)$. [Maskin & Riley (2000)](https://www.isid.ac.in/~dmishra/topicsdoc/maskin_riley.pdf "RES 67(3): 413–438, Propositions 3.3–3.5"). Inefficiency is $O(\varepsilon^2)$ under weak asymmetry ([Fibich & Gavious 2003](http://www.math.tau.ac.il/~fibich/Manuscripts/first_rev_final.pdf "MOR 28(4): 836–852, Section 5.4")).
- 发现 6: **Q-function mapping quantifies misallocation.** $Q(v)$ defined by $\phi_s(b) = Q(\phi_w(b))$ satisfies $Q(v) > v$: the strong bidder has valuation $Q(v) > v$ when tied with the weak bidder at valuation $v$. Width $Q(v) - v$ measures local severity of potential misallocation. [Maskin & Riley (2000)](https://www.isid.ac.in/~dmishra/topicsdoc/maskin_riley.pdf "RES 67(3): 413–438, eq. (4.1)").
- 发现 7: **Strong bidder shades more, weak bidder shades less (Proposition 3.5).** Under CSD: $\phi_s(b) > \phi_w(b)$ for all interior $b$. At the same valuation, the strong bidder bids lower. First-order perturbation confirms: $B_i > 0$ (more aggressive) for weaker bidder, $B_i < 0$ (less aggressive) for stronger bidder. [Maskin & Riley (2000)](https://www.isid.ac.in/~dmishra/topicsdoc/maskin_riley.pdf "RES 67(3): 413–438, Prop. 3.5(ii)"); [Fibich & Gavious (2003)](http://www.math.tau.ac.il/~fibich/Manuscripts/first_rev_final.pdf "MOR 28(4): 836–852, Prop. 1").
- 发现 8: **Bidder preferences across formats (Proposition 3.6).** Under CSD: strong buyer strictly prefers SPA; weak buyer prefers FPSB. Intuition: in FPSB, the weak bidder faces less aggressive competition (strong bidder shades more), improving the weak bidder's surplus. [Maskin & Riley (2000)](https://www.isid.ac.in/~dmishra/topicsdoc/maskin_riley.pdf "RES 67(3): 413–438, Prop. 3.6").
- 发现 9: **Myerson (1981) optimal auction.** Revenue-maximizing mechanism allocates to highest virtual valuation $c_i(t_i) = t_i - (1-F_i(t_i))/f_i(t_i)$, using bidder-specific reserve prices $z_i = c_i^{-1}(t_0)$. Standard FPSB without bidder-specific reserves is generically suboptimal under asymmetry. [Myerson (1981)](https://cramton.umd.edu/market-design-papers/myerson-optimal-auction-design.pdf "MOR 6(1): 58–73, Sections 5–6").
- 发现 10: **FPSB partially implements optimal tilt.** [Kirkegaard (2012)](https://www.econometricsociety.org/uploads/Supmat/9859_extensions.pdf "Supplement to Econometrica 80(5): 2349–2364") shows FPSB inherently favors the weak bidder moderately, partially implementing the Myerson-optimal allocation tilt. Under reverse hazard rate dominance, SPA is the worst "winner-pay" format for revenue. Gap between FPSB with common optimal reserve and Myerson optimal is $O(\varepsilon^2)$ for weak asymmetry, $O(\varepsilon^2/n^3)$ for large $n$.

### 关键发现
- 发现 1: **Government procurement.** [Flambard & Perrigne (2006)](https://ideas.repec.org/a/ecj/econjl/v116y2006i514p1014-1036.html "Economic Journal 116(514): 1014–1036") studied Montréal snow removal FPSB auctions, classifying firms by geographic proximity (West vs. Non-West) as natural source of cost asymmetry. Structural estimation extending GPV recovered significantly different cost distributions. [Lamy (2011)](https://shs.hal.science/halshs-00586039/document "PSE WP 2008-64") used these CDFs for Monte Carlo calibration of a symmetry test.
- 发现 2: **Oil & gas lease auctions.** [Hendricks & Porter (1988)](https://users.ssc.wisc.edu/~khendricks2/publications/Information&Returns.pdf "AER 78(5): 865–883") documented informational asymmetry on OCS drainage tracts (neighbor vs. non-neighbor firms). [Campo, Perrigne & Vuong (2003)](https://ideas.repec.org/a/jae/japmet/v18y2003i2p179-207.html "J. Appl. Econ. 18(2): 179–207") extended GPV to asymmetric affiliated private values on OCS wildcat auction data with two bidder classes.
- 发现 3: **Timber auctions.** [Athey, Levin & Seira (2011)](https://web.stanford.edu/~jdlevin/Papers/Auctions.pdf "QJE 126(1): 207–257") found sealed-bid (FP) auctions attract more small bidders and can generate higher revenue than open auctions — consistent with Maskin–Riley (2000) prediction that weak bidders prefer FPSB.
- 发现 4: **Spectrum auctions.** Incumbent vs. entrant asymmetry in FCC/European auctions. [Fox & Bajari (2013)](https://www.nber.org/system/files/working_papers/w11671/w11671.pdf "NBER WP 11671") provided structural model noting asymmetric valuations as first-order feature of spectrum markets.
- 发现 5: **Online advertising.** Google completed transition to unified first-price auctions in September 2019. [Despotakis, Ravi & Sayedi (2019)](https://faculty.washington.edu/aminsa/papers/fpa.pdf "First-price Auctions in Online Display Advertising") showed first-price is the unique equilibrium format under header bidding. Asymmetry among advertisers is a first-order feature; bid-shading algorithms must account for heterogeneous competition.
- 发现 6: **GPV (2000) identification.** [Guerre, Perrigne & Vuong (2000)](https://kylewoodward.com/blog-data/pdfs/references/guerre+perrigne+vuong-econometrica-2000A.pdf "Econometrica 68(3): 525–574") established the foundational structural estimation framework: the FOC inverts to $v_i = b_i + \frac{G(b_i)}{(I-1)g(b_i)}$, recovering private values from observed bids without solving the equilibrium ODE. Two-step kernel estimator achieves optimal convergence rate. Under asymmetry with observed identities, class-specific bid distributions $G_i$ are estimated separately.
- 发现 7: **Anonymous asymmetric bidders.** [Lamy (2011)](https://shs.hal.science/halshs-00586039/document "J. Econometrics 167(1): 113–132, WP 2008-64, Prop. 3.1") showed asymmetric IPV is identified under anonymous bidding via polynomial root-finding on order-statistic CDFs. Asymmetric affiliated private values are NOT identified under anonymity.
- 发现 8: **N > 2 bidders.** System expands to $N$ coupled ODEs with $2N$ boundary conditions. Backward-shooting instability worsens: $v_{\min} \geq F^{-1}(\sqrt[N-1]{|\varepsilon|})$; for $N=100$ and machine precision, $v_{\min} \approx 0.70$. Fibich–Gavish boundary-value method scales to $N=450$. Existence and uniqueness for $N$ bidders established by Lebrun (1999, 2006).
- 发现 9: **Risk aversion.** Maskin & Riley (2000b) proved existence for risk-averse asymmetric bidders. CRRA utility complicates the ODE by coupling risk-aversion parameters with distributional asymmetry. Joint identification of risk aversion and asymmetry requires additional assumptions.
- 发现 10: **Discrete/finite-type approximations.** [Athey (2001)](https://kylewoodward.com/blog-data/pdfs/references/athey-econometrica-2001A.pdf "Econometrica 69(4): 861–889") proved existence via finite-action limit; [Au et al. (2021)](https://ideas.repec.org/a/inm/ordeca/v18y2021i4p321-334.html "Decision Analysis 18(4): 321–334") exploit this constructively in the BID algorithm.
- 发现 11: **State of the art (2026): what is settled.** Existence (Lebrun 1996/1999, Maskin–Riley 2000b, Athey 2001), uniqueness (Lebrun 2006, Maskin–Riley 2003), ODE characterization (Plum 1992, Maskin–Riley 2000), closed-form solutions (Kaplan–Zamir 2012, Plum 1992, Cheng 2006), perturbation (Fibich–Gavious 2003), numerical solvers (Fibich–Gavish 2011, $O(h^4)$ accuracy), and structural estimation (GPV 2000, Campo–Perrigne–Vuong 2003, Lamy 2011).
- 发现 12: **What remains challenging.** (a) No formal convergence proof for any numerical method ([HKP 2012](https://vinci.cs.uiowa.edu/~hjp/download/hkp.pdf "Using Economic Theory to Guide Numerical Analysis")). (b) Large-$N$ with genuinely heterogeneous distributions is computationally expensive. (c) Joint identification of risk aversion and asymmetry. (d) Multi-unit, combinatorial, and interdependent-value extensions largely open.
- 发现 13: **Direct answer to the title question.** There is no single closed-form formula for arbitrary $(F_1, F_2)$. However, the equilibrium is fully characterized by a well-posed ODE-BVP (the inverse-bid system) that can be solved numerically to arbitrary precision — particularly via the Fibich–Gavish (2011) boundary-value method with $O(h^4)$ accuracy. Closed-form solutions exist for important families (uniform: Kaplan–Zamir 2012; power-function: Plum 1992; power distributions: Cheng 2006). For weakly asymmetric distributions, the Fibich–Gavious (2003) perturbation expansion provides an analytical first-order approximation accurate even for moderate asymmetry. The equilibrium exists and is unique under standard regularity conditions. The problem is thus "fully solved" in the sense that a complete theoretical characterization and practical computational tools are available.
