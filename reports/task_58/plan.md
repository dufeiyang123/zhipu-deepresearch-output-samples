# Section 1：章节研究计划

## Chapter 1: Conceptual Foundations — Horizontal Gene Transfer Beyond Microbes
### 研究目标
- What is horizontal gene transfer (HGT), how was it discovered and characterized in prokaryotes, and why was it long assumed to be negligible in multicellular eukaryotes?
- Trace the intellectual shift from "HGT is a prokaryotic phenomenon" to recognition that it also operates—albeit rarely—in plants and animals.
- Provide a timeline of landmark discoveries that challenged the prokaryote-only view.
- Establish scope: how many eukaryotic HGT events are now cataloged?

### 关键发现
- 发现 1: The four foundational milestones of prokaryotic HGT are transformation (Griffith 1928), identification of DNA as the transforming agent (Avery et al. 1944), conjugation (Lederberg & Tatum 1946), and transduction (Zinder & Lederberg 1952). [Daubin & Szöllősi 2016](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4817804/ "Cold Spring Harbor Perspectives in Biology — HGT and the History of Life")
- 发现 2: The Weismann barrier, proposed by August Weismann in 1892, postulates strict unidirectional information flow from germline to soma, and was long invoked to explain why HGT should be negligible in multicellular animals. Critically, it does not apply to plants, where germ cells arise late from somatic meristems. [Weismann barrier — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Weismann_barrier "Verified against Weismann 1892; Whitham & Slobodchikoff 1981 on plant somatic mutations")
- 发现 3: Obligate intracellular endosymbionts (e.g., *Wolbachia*) circumvent the Weismann barrier by residing within germline cells, providing direct physical access for DNA transfer to host genomes. [Keeling & Palmer 2008](https://www.nature.com/articles/nrg2386 "Nature Reviews Genetics 9: 605–618")
- 发现 4: HGT and LGT are synonymous terms; horizontal transposon transfer (HTT) is a specific subcategory; endosymbiotic gene transfer (EGT) refers to organelle-to-nucleus transfers and is conceptually distinct from HGT sensu stricto. [Schaack, Gilbert & Feschotte 2010](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Horizontal_gene_transfer "Citing Schaack et al. 2010, Trends in Ecology & Evolution 25: 537–546")
- 发现 5: Timeline of landmark eukaryotic HGT discoveries: (a) 1983 — White et al. found *Agrobacterium* T-DNA (cT-DNA) in *Nicotiana glauca*, the first bacterium-to-plant natural gene transfer (*Nature* 301: 348–350) [Matveeva & Lutova 2014](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4127661/ "Review confirming White et al. 1983"); (b) 2002 — Kondo et al. documented a >11 kb *Wolbachia* genome fragment on the X chromosome of the adzuki bean beetle (*PNAS* 99: 14280–14285) [Kondo et al. 2002](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC137875/ "PNAS original paper"); (c) 2007 — Dunning Hotopp et al. showed widespread *Wolbachia*-to-eukaryote transfers in 4 insect and 4 nematode species, up to nearly the entire ~1 Mb *Wolbachia* genome (*Science* 317: 1753–1756) [Dunning Hotopp et al. 2007](https://www.science.org/doi/10.1126/science.1142490 "Science original paper"); (d) 2008 — Gladyshev et al. reported massive HGT in bdelloid rotifers, ~8–10% foreign genes (*Science* 320: 1210–1213) [Gladyshev et al. 2008](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/18511688/ "Science original paper"); (e) 2010 — Moran & Jarvik showed fungal-origin carotenoid biosynthesis genes in pea aphids (*Science* 328: 624–627) [Moran & Jarvik 2010](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/20431015/ "Science original paper"); (f) 2014 — Li et al. demonstrated ferns acquired neochrome from hornworts ~180 Mya (*PNAS* 111: 6672–6677) [Li et al. 2014](https://www.pnas.org/doi/10.1073/pnas.1319929111 "PNAS original paper"); (g) 2015 — Kyndt et al. found *Agrobacterium* T-DNA in all 291 tested cultivated sweet potato accessions (*PNAS* 112: 5844–5849), making it a "natural GMO" [Kyndt et al. 2015](https://www.pnas.org/doi/10.1073/pnas.1419685112 "PNAS original paper"); (h) 2015 — Crisp et al. concluded HGT expression is a hallmark of both vertebrate and invertebrate genomes (*Genome Biology* 16: 50) [Crisp et al. 2015](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Horizontal_gene_transfer "Wikipedia citing Crisp et al. 2015"); (i) 2022 — Li et al. identified 1,410 HGT-acquired genes across 218 insect genomes (*Cell* 185: 2975–2987) [Li et al. 2022](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/366238079 "ResearchGate confirmation of Cell paper").
- 发现 6: The most comprehensive compendium of metazoan HGT to date is Spaulding & Fierst (2025), cataloging 9,511 protein-coding genes identified as horizontally transferred to metazoans from bacteria, fungi, archaea, and protists. Note: this is a bioRxiv preprint pending peer review. [Spaulding & Fierst 2025](https://www.biorxiv.org/content/10.1101/2025.11.05.686818v1 "Preprint: 9,511 HGT protein-coding genes in metazoans")
- 发现 7: Bdelloid rotifers hold the record for the highest proportion of horizontally acquired genes among animals (~8–10%), with HGT ancient, ongoing, and more frequent in species from desiccating habitats. [Eyres et al. 2015](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4632278/ "BMC Biology — HGT in bdelloid rotifers")
- 发现 8: The 2008 Keeling & Palmer review in *Nature Reviews Genetics* (9: 605–618) remains the foundational synthesis on eukaryotic HGT, and Daubin & Szöllősi (2016) underscored that "horizontal gene transfer has been extensive throughout the evolution of the eukaryotic domain." [Keeling & Palmer 2008](https://www.nature.com/articles/nrg2386 "NRG landmark review") [Daubin & Szöllősi 2016](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4817804/ "Cold Spring Harbor Perspectives")

## Chapter 2: Mechanisms and Detection — How Genes Cross Species Boundaries in Eukaryotes
### 研究目标
- Through what biological routes do foreign genes enter and integrate into eukaryotic genomes?
- How do researchers distinguish genuine HGT from artifacts (contamination, incomplete lineage sorting, convergent evolution)?
- What are the strengths and limitations of current detection pipelines?

### 关键发现
- 发现 1: Parasitic plant haustoria are major HGT conduits. Yang et al. (2016) identified 52 high-confidence HGT events across 42 gene families in three parasitic Orobanchaceae species, with HGT frequency increasing with parasitic dependence (1 event in free-living *Lindenbergia* vs. 34 gene families in holoparasite *Phelipanche aegyptiaca*). The majority (24/28 analyzable) were mediated by genomic DNA fragments (intron conservation), not mRNA. HGT genes are preferentially expressed in haustoria and under purifying selection. [Yang et al. 2016](https://www.pnas.org/doi/10.1073/pnas.1608765113 "PNAS 113:E7010–E7019")
- 发现 2: Kim et al. (2014) demonstrated genomic-scale mRNA exchange between *Cuscuta pentagona* and its hosts (*Arabidopsis*, *Solanum*), with thousands of mRNA species moving bidirectionally through haustorial connections, providing a plausible mRNA-mediated HGT route. [Kim et al. 2014](https://www.science.org/doi/10.1126/science.1253122 "Science 345:808–811")
- 发现 3: Vogel et al. (2018) identified 108 transcribed and likely functional HGT events in *Cuscuta campestris*, confirming extensive gene acquisition by parasitic plants. [Vogel et al. 2018](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/31332314/ "PNAS 115:E9793–E9802")
- 发现 4: *Wolbachia* endosymbiont-to-host transfer is mechanistically enabled by the endosymbiont's intracellular residence within germline cells. Dunning Hotopp et al. (2007) found nearly the entire *Wolbachia* genome (~1 Mb) integrated into *D. ananassae* nuclear genome; some transferred genes are transcribed. [Dunning Hotopp et al. 2007](https://www.science.org/doi/10.1126/science.1142490 "Science 317:1753–1756")
- 发现 5: Transposable elements serve as both cargo and vehicles for HGT. *Mavericks/Polintons* are ancient virus-like TEs that acquired a herpesvirus-like fusogen (MFUS-1), enabling widespread exchange of cargo genes between divergent nematode species spanning hundreds of millions of years. [Widen et al. 2023](https://www.science.org/doi/10.1126/science.ade0705 "Science 380:eade0705")
- 发现 6: BovB, a ~3.2 kb LINE retrotransposon, has undergone at least 9 independent horizontal transfer events across vertebrates and arthropods, contributing up to 18.4% of the cow genome. Reptile tick species were identified as plausible vectors. [Walsh et al. 2013](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3549140/ "PNAS 110:1012–1016")
- 发现 7: Starship elements are giant transposons (avg ~110 kb, up to >700 kb) in Pezizomycotina fungi, mobilized by "Captain" tyrosine recombinases, carrying cargo genes for metal tolerance, xenobiotic degradation, and secondary metabolism. [Gluck-Thaler et al. 2023](https://www.pnas.org/doi/10.1073/pnas.2214521120 "PNAS 120:e2214521120")
- 发现 8: Viruses serve as HGT vectors: baculoviruses mediate insect transposon transfer [Gilbert et al. 2014](https://www.nature.com/articles/ncomms4348 "Nat Commun 5:3348"); poxviruses may transfer retroposons between reptiles and mammals [Piskurek & Okada 2007](https://www.pnas.org/doi/10.1073/pnas.0700531104 "PNAS 104:12046–12051").
- 发现 9: The "weak-link model" (Huang 2013) proposes that organisms with accessible germ-line cells (early embryos, organisms without sequestered germ lines, resting stages) are most susceptible to HGT. [Huang 2013](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1002/bies.201300007 "BioEssays 35:868–875")
- 发现 10: **Detection tools**: HGTphyloDetect achieves 98.16% accuracy, 87.57% sensitivity, 98.49% specificity using Alien Index (AI ≥ 45) combined with phylogenetic validation [Yuan et al. 2023](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10025432/ "Brief Bioinform 24:bbad035"). Alienness uses AI scoring validated on plant-parasitic nematodes (AI > 14 eliminates false positives) [Rancurel et al. 2017](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC5664098/ "Genes 8:248"). Compositional methods (GC content, codon usage) are unreliable for ancient HGT due to amelioration [Koski et al. 2001](https://academic.oup.com/mbe/article/18/3/404/1070883 "Mol Biol Evol 18:404–412").
- 发现 11: **Tardigrade genome controversy**: Boothby et al. (2015) claimed 17.5% (6,663/39,532 genes) of *H. dujardini* genome was HGT-derived [Boothby et al. 2015](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/26598659/ "PNAS 112:15976–15981"). Koutsovoulos et al. (2016) resequenced the genome (~110 Mb vs. original bloated assembly), identified extensive contamination via GC-coverage blobplots, and found only 94 strong HGT candidates (0.4%), comparable to other eukaryotes [Koutsovoulos et al. 2016](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4983863/ "PNAS 113:5053–5058"). Richards & Monier (2016) proposed six guidelines for rigorous HGT identification [Richards & Monier 2016](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4983848/ "PNAS 113:4892–4893").
- 发现 12: **Major sources of false positives**: (a) insufficient taxon sampling (71% of artifacts in Yang et al. 2016); (b) contamination from co-cultured organisms; (c) frame-shift errors and assembly artifacts; (d) incomplete lineage sorting; (e) compositional amelioration masking ancient HGT. [Yang et al. 2016](https://www.pnas.org/doi/10.1073/pnas.1608765113 "False positive analysis") [Ravenhall et al. 2015](https://journals.plos.org/ploscompbiol/article?id=10.1371/journal.pcbi.1004095 "PLoS Comput Biol 11:e1004095")

## Chapter 3: Horizontal Gene Transfer in Plants — From Organellar Exchange to Nuclear Acquisition
### 研究目标
- How prevalent is HGT across plant lineages? What types of genes are transferred and from what donors?
- Ecological/morphological contexts facilitating plant HGT (parasitism, mycorrhizal symbiosis).
- Parasitic plant systems as hotspots; interkingdom HGT; naturally transgenic plants; fern neochrome.
- Evidence for adaptive value of transferred genes; 2025–2026 reassessments of iHGT scale.

### 关键发现
- 发现 1: **Mitochondrial HGT is widespread in angiosperms.** Bergthorsson et al. (2003) showed "evolutionarily frequent" horizontal transfer of standard mitochondrial genes between distantly related flowering plants [Bergthorsson et al. 2003](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/12853958/ "Nature 424:197–201"). *Amborella trichopoda* acquired foreign copies of 20 of its 31 known mitochondrial protein genes (26 foreign genes total), from other angiosperms and at least three moss donors [Bergthorsson et al. 2004](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/15598737/ "PNAS 101:17747–17752"). The mitochondrial *cox1* group I intron experienced at least 80 plant-to-plant horizontal transfers among 833 angiosperm species following a single initial fungal "seed transfer" [Yang et al. 2016](https://www.pnas.org/doi/10.1073/pnas.1608765113 "citing Sanchez-Puerta et al. 2008 and Cho et al. 1998").
- 发现 2: ***Cuscuta* (dodder) harbors 108 functional HGT genes** contributing to haustoria structure, defense, and amino acid metabolism. One stolen gene produces microRNAs sent back into the host to silence defense genes. Eighteen of these 108 genes appear in all dodder species, indicating acquisition by the ancestral dodder [Penn State University](https://www.psu.edu/news/research/story/parasitic-plants-use-stolen-genes-make-them-better-parasites "Yang et al. 2019, Nature Plants") [ScienceDaily](https://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2019/07/190722182130.htm "108 functional HGT genes in dodder").
- 发现 3: **Orobanchaceae HGT scales with parasitic dependence.** Yang et al. (2016) identified 52 high-confidence HGT events across 42 gene families: 1 in free-living *Lindenbergia*, 2 in facultative hemiparasite *Triphysaria*, 10 in obligate hemiparasite *Striga*, 34 in holoparasite *Phelipanche*. HGT genes are preferentially expressed in haustoria and under purifying selection. [Yang et al. 2016](https://www.pnas.org/doi/10.1073/pnas.1608765113 "PNAS 113:E7010–E7019")
- 发现 4: **First nuclear HGT in *Striga***: Yoshida et al. (2010) showed a nuclear monocot gene moved from host into the eudicot parasite *Striga hermonthica*, with a poly-A-like sequence suggesting mRNA-mediated transfer [Yoshida et al. 2010](https://www.science.org/doi/10.1126/science.1187145 "Science 328:1128"). The albumin 1 xenogene has been maintained through >150 speciation events over ~16 Mya in parasitic Orobanchaceae.
- 发现 5: **Naturally transgenic plants are rare but confirmed.** Kyndt et al. (2015) found two *Agrobacterium* T-DNA regions in all 291 cultivated sweet potato accessions tested (*PNAS* 112:5844–5849) [Kyndt et al. 2015](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/25902487/ "PNAS 112:5844–5849"). At least 15 *Nicotiana* species contain cT-DNA from *A. rhizogenes* [Matveeva & Lutova 2014](https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/plant-science/articles/10.3389/fpls.2014.00326/full "Frontiers Plant Sci. 5:326"). *Linaria vulgaris* also harbors T-DNA but with pseudogenized *rol* genes. A screen of 127 dicot species from 38 families found T-DNA only in *Linaria*, indicating that germline fixation is rare.
- 发现 6: **Fern neochrome acquired from hornworts ~179 Mya via HGT.** Li et al. (2014) showed fern neochromes are phylogenetically nested within hornwort neochromes (alternative hypotheses rejected at *P* < 10⁻³⁰). This chimeric photoreceptor (phytochrome + phototropin) may have enabled fern diversification "in the shadow of angiosperms" by optimizing low-light phototropism. Evidence for recurrent fern-to-fern HGT of neochrome was also found. [Li et al. 2014](https://www.pnas.org/doi/10.1073/pnas.1319929111 "PNAS 111:6672–6677")
- 发现 7: **Fungi-to-plant HGT confers disease resistance.** The *Fhb7* gene was horizontally transferred from endophytic fungus *Epichloë* to wheat relative *Thinopyrum elongatum*, encoding a GST that detoxifies Fusarium trichothecene toxins; it has been introgressed into bread wheat cultivars [Wang et al. 2020 via Azad et al. 2025](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12451028/ "citing Wang et al. 2020, Science 368:eaba5435"). Shinozuka et al. (2017) documented β-1,6-glucanase transfer from fungal endophyte to perennial ryegrass. Kfoury et al. (2024) showed multiple HGT events shaped plant glycosyl hydrolase diversity.
- 发现 8: **Grass *Alloteropsis semialata* acquired 59 functional genes** for photosynthesis, disease resistance, and stress tolerance from at least nine donor grass species. Species with rhizomes acquired more genes, likely due to increased germline exposure [Hibdige et al. 2021 via Azad et al. 2025](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12451028/ "citing Hibdige et al. 2021, New Phytol 230:2474–2486").
- 发现 9: **C₄ photosynthesis genes were laterally transferred between grasses**, contributing to repeated independent evolution of C₄ photosynthesis — one of the most significant plant adaptive innovations [Christin et al. 2012 via Li et al. 2014](https://www.pnas.org/doi/10.1073/pnas.1319929111 "citing Christin et al. 2012, Curr Biol 22:445–449").
- 发现 10: **2025 reassessment dramatically reduces iHGT estimates.** Aguirre-Carvajal & Armijos-Jaramillo (2025) reanalyzed 1,170 previously reported iHGT candidates in Plantae: only 29.3% (343) retained phylogenetic signals consistent with iHGT (19.66% HGT, 9.66% shifted recipient lineage); 36.84% classified as NoHGT, 30.6% inconclusive, 2.14% contamination, 1.11% misclassified EGT. Key drivers of overestimation: expanded databases revealing eukaryotic homologs, contamination, EGT misclassification, insufficient taxon sampling. [Aguirre-Carvajal & Armijos-Jaramillo 2025](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1002/ece3.72653?af=R "Ecology and Evolution 15:e72653")
- 发现 11: **No evidence that mycorrhizal networks facilitate plant-to-plant DNA transfer.** HGT *to* mycorrhizal fungi is documented (Li et al. 2018, *Frontiers Plant Sci.* 9:701), but plant-to-plant transfer via mycorrhizae remains undemonstrated. [Li et al. 2018](https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/plant-science/articles/10.3389/fpls.2018.00701/full "Frontiers Plant Sci. 9:701")
- 发现 12: **Yue et al. (2012) proposed 57 gene families horizontally transferred to land plant ancestors**, encoding enzymes for starch biosynthesis, hormone metabolism, xylem formation, and defense — critical for the aquatic-to-terrestrial transition. However, the 2025 reassessment suggests many may need re-evaluation [via Azad et al. 2025](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12451028/ "citing Yue et al. 2012, Nat Commun 3:1152").

## Chapter 4: Horizontal Gene Transfer in Animals — Endosymbionts, Parasites, and Unexpected Acquisitions
### 研究目标
- Which animal lineages show the most convincing evidence of HGT? What are the donor sources?
- Are transferred genes functional and do they confer adaptive advantages?
- How does the frequency and significance of animal HGT compare with that in plants?

### 关键发现
- 发现 1: ***Wolbachia*-to-host nuclear transfers (NUWTs) are widespread but mostly nonfunctional.** *D. ananassae* acquired nearly the entire ~1.4 Mb *Wolbachia* genome. The cattle lungworm *Dictyocaulus viviparus* harbors ~1 Mb of *Wolbachia*-like insertions (567 identifiable genes), but nearly all are pseudogenized — only 6/61,134 transcripts showed *Wolbachia*-like expression. NUWTs reveal "palaeosymbioses" in filarial nematodes (*Onchocerca*, *Loa loa*) that lost live *Wolbachia* infections. [Koutsovoulos et al. 2014](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4046930/ "PLoS Genetics 10:e1004397")
- 发现 2: **Bdelloid rotifers harbor 8–14% non-metazoan genes — the highest documented in any animal.** Foreign gene proportions range from 9.5% (*R. socialis*) to 14.1% (*R. tardigrada*); gain rate ~12.8 genes per lineage per Mya vs. 2.0 losses. Desiccation-tolerant species harbor more foreign genes (supporting the hypothesis that desiccation-induced DNA breaks facilitate environmental DNA incorporation), but permanently aquatic species still acquire hundreds of unique foreign genes. Foreign genes encode metabolic enzymes for degrading plant/fungal/bacterial cell walls. [Eyres et al. 2015](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4632278/ "BMC Biology 13:90")
- 发现 3: **Nowell et al. (2018) confirmed high HGT is unique to bdelloids among protostomes** (11.7–14.5% vs. max 3.6% in non-bdelloid annelid *Capitella teleta*). ~80% of HGT genes are shared across all four bdelloid species, indicating ancient acquisition. The desiccation-tolerant tardigrade *R. varieornatus* and chironomid *P. vanderplanki* both showed only ~1% HGT, suggesting desiccation alone does not explain elevated bdelloid HGT — long-term asexuality may be the key factor. [Nowell et al. 2018](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC5916493/ "PLoS Biology 16:e2004830")
- 发现 4: **Li et al. (2022) identified 1,410 HGT genes from 741 transfer events across 218 insect genomes.** Donors: 79% bacteria, 13.8% fungi, 3% plants, 2.6% viruses. Lepidoptera had the most HGT genes (avg 16/species), followed by Hemiptera (13), Coleoptera (6). Of 1,410 genes, 849 gained 1,534 introns *post-transfer* (88% repeat-derived), and HGT genes with introns showed ~11-fold higher expression. Strong correlation (r = 0.68) between HGT donor genera and known insect symbiont genera, with *Wolbachia* accounting for ~3% of donors. [Li et al. 2022](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9357157/ "Cell 185:2975–2987")
- 发现 5: **CRISPR-validated: a *Listeria*-derived gene enhances male courtship in Lepidoptera.** Knockout of *LOC105383139* in diamondback moth reduced courtship index from 84–86% to 46–48% and mating index from 64–65% to 10–13%, yielding ~5–6-fold fewer offspring. The gene is present in nearly all sampled lepidopterans with male-biased expression. [Li et al. 2022](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9357157/ "Cell 185:2975–2987")
- 发现 6: **Fungal carotenoid genes in aphids and spider mites — the first animals capable of de novo carotenoid synthesis.** Pea aphids acquired carotenoid desaturase and cyclase–synthase genes from fungi, underlying the red–green color polymorphism affecting predator interactions [Moran & Jarvik 2010](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/20431015/ "Science 328:624–627"). Spider mites independently acquired homologous genes; red morphs show 3–6-fold higher expression [Altincicek et al. 2012](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3297373/ "Biology Letters 8:253–257").
- 发现 7: **Bacterial cdtB toxin gene domesticated by insects.** cdtB (DNase I toxin) was horizontally transferred from APSE-2 bacteriophage into Drosophilidae (3 independent events, 7–24 Ma) and Aphididae (1 event, ~41 Ma). Insect cdtB retains functional DNase activity (higher than *E. coli* CdtB), is under purifying selection, and is most highly expressed during the parasitoid-vulnerable larval stage. In *D. bipectinata*, a cdtB::aip56 fusion gene was found, suggesting co-transfer from phage. [Verster et al. 2019](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC6759069/ "MBE 36:2105–2110")
- 发现 8: **Tardigrade HGT consensus: ~0.4–1% genuine HGT**, comparable to most protostomes. The 17.5% claim (Boothby et al. 2015) was an artifact of contamination. *R. varieornatus* possesses the tardigrade-unique Dsup protein that suppresses X-ray-induced DNA damage by ~40%, but this is not necessarily HGT-derived. [Koutsovoulos et al. 2016](https://www.pnas.org/doi/10.1073/pnas.1600338113 "PNAS 113:5053–5058") [Nowell et al. 2018](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC5916493/ "PLoS Biology 16:e2004830")
- 发现 9: **Vertebrate HGT is dominated by transposable element transfers and ERV domestication.** BovB LINE retrotransposons underwent at least 9 independent horizontal transfers across vertebrates and arthropods, with ticks as plausible vectors [Walsh et al. 2013](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/23277587/ "PNAS 110:1012–1016"). SPIN transposons show ~96% identity among 7 distantly related vertebrate species [Pace et al. 2008](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/18936483/ "PNAS 105:17023–17028"). HERVs comprise ~8% of the human genome; syncytin-1 and syncytin-2 (from HERV-W and HERV-FRD) are essential for placental formation [Hayward et al. 2015](https://www.pnas.org/doi/10.1073/pnas.1414980112 "PNAS 112:464–469").
- 发现 10: **Functional non-TE gene HGT into vertebrate genomes remains unconfirmed.** Salzberg (2017) systematically rebutted Crisp et al.'s (2015) claim of 145 HGT genes in the human genome: 12/17 original claims and 26/28 new claims failed re-examination using updated databases. Current consensus: beyond ERV domestication, no bacterial-to-vertebrate functional gene transfer has been convincingly demonstrated. [Salzberg 2017](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/316944848_Horizontal_gene_transfer_is_not_a_hallmark_of_the_human_genome "Genome Biology 18:85")
- 发现 11: **Cross-kingdom comparison: animal vs. plant HGT.** Plants show extremely common organellar HGT and substantial nuclear HGT (especially parasitic plants). Animals show lower overall frequency, but bdelloid rotifers (8–14%) exceed most plant nuclear HGT. Insect HGT is broad but variable. In vertebrates, TE horizontal transfer is well-documented but functional non-TE gene transfer is rare/contentious.

## Chapter 5: Evolutionary Significance — Rarity, Adaptation, and the Eukaryotic Tree of Life
### 研究目标
- Is HGT in eukaryotes merely a curiosity, or a significant evolutionary force?
- Under what ecological circumstances does HGT provide an adaptive edge?
- How does eukaryotic HGT reshape understanding of the tree of life vs. a web/network of life?
- What patterns emerge regarding which genes are successfully transferred and retained?
- The "ratchet" model: why rarity does not negate significance.
- Gene categories preferentially transferred (metabolic enzymes, defense, detoxification, stress response).
- Implications for phylogenetic reconstruction and reticulate evolution.

### 关键发现
- 发现 1: **Gene transfer ratchet model.** Doolittle (1998) proposed that early phagotrophic eukaryotes inevitably lysed bacterial prey near their nuclear DNA, providing continuous opportunities for bacterial gene integration; once a bacterial gene replaces a host homolog, the process is irreversible — constituting a ratchet. [Doolittle 1998](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/9724962/ "Trends in Genetics 14:307-311")
- 发现 2: **Replacement vs. additive HGT.** Soucy, Huang & Gogarten (2015) formalized two HGT categories: replacement HGT (foreign gene displaces native homolog) and additive HGT (foreign gene introduces a new function). Additive HGT is particularly significant because a single transfer can open entirely new ecological niches — making HGT frequency a poor proxy for HGT significance. [Soucy et al. 2015](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/26184597/ "Nature Reviews Genetics 16:472-482")
- 发现 3: **Complexity hypothesis.** Jain, Rivera & Lake (1999) showed that informational genes (transcription, translation) are far less frequently transferred than operational genes (metabolism, housekeeping). Among 312 orthologous gene sets across six prokaryotic genomes, operational gene trees were significantly more topologically diverse than informational ones (mean distance 2.3 vs. 1.2 steps, P < 0.0003). A protein with 6 interaction partners has a transfer success probability of ~0.25⁶ ≈ 0.00024. [Jain et al. 1999](https://www.pnas.org/doi/10.1073/pnas.96.7.3801 "PNAS 96:3801-3806")
- 发现 4: **Functional classification of bacteria-to-eukaryote HGT.** Husnik & McCutcheon (2018) classified functional HGT events into maintenance transfers (replacing genes lost from degenerating endosymbiont genomes) and innovation transfers (providing genuinely new functions). Innovation transfers include degradation of plant carbohydrates, defense against bacterial pathogens via peptidoglycan-degrading enzymes (independently acquired in ≥3 eukaryotic supergroups), survival in extreme environments, amino acid/cofactor biosynthesis, and anaerobic metabolism. [Husnik & McCutcheon 2018](http://mccutcheonlab.org/s/17_husnik_nrm.pdf "Nature Reviews Microbiology 16:67-79")
- 发现 5: **Ecological contexts ranked by HGT evidence volume.** (1) Obligate endosymbiosis — ~15–20% of nuclear genes in photosynthetic eukaryotes from plastid-derived EGT; bacterial genes outnumber archaeal genes in yeast ~3:1 [Ku et al. 2015](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4547308/ "PNAS 112:10139-10146"). (2) Parasitism — HGT frequency scales with parasitic dependence (Yang et al. 2016). (3) Intracellular bacterial symbionts — strong r = 0.68 correlation between HGT donor genera and known insect symbiont genera (Li et al. 2022). (4) Phagotrophy — rumen ciliates ~4% bacterial genes. (5) Environmental DNA uptake via desiccation — bdelloid desiccation-tolerant species 12.8–14.1% vs. aquatic 9.5–10.4%.
- 发现 6: **Prokaryotic vs. eukaryotic HGT frequency.** ~81% of genes in prokaryotic genomes have been involved in HGT at some point; in contrast, eukaryotic HGT (excluding EGT) accounts for ~0% in human genome (Salzberg 2017), ~1% in tardigrades, ~8–14% in bdelloid rotifers, ~4% in rumen ciliates, and typically <1–2% in most other multicellular eukaryotes — an order-of-magnitude difference. [Ku et al. 2015](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4547308/ "PNAS 112:10139-10146")
- 发现 7: **Despite lower frequency, individual eukaryotic HGT events can rival prokaryotic HGT in functional impact.** Prokaryotic HGT often involves operational genes with transient selective advantages; eukaryotic HGT events such as carotenoid biosynthesis in aphids, neochrome in ferns, and Fhb7 in wheat have been retained over tens to hundreds of millions of years. [Keeling & Palmer 2008](http://www.ask-force.org/web/HorizontalGT/Keeling-HGT-Eukaryotic-Evolution-2008.pdf "Nature Reviews Genetics 9:605-618")
- 发现 8: **Tree vs. web of life.** Doolittle (1999) challenged the universal tree concept: "the history of life cannot properly be represented as a tree." [Doolittle 1999](https://www.science.org/doi/10.1126/science.284.5423.2124 "Science 284:2124-2129") Rivera & Lake (2004) proposed the "ring of life" model showing the eukaryotic genome originated from a fusion between two prokaryotic lineages. [Rivera & Lake 2004](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/15356622/ "Nature 431:152-155") Soucy et al. (2015) argued HGT shapes "the web of life," but for eukaryotes a tree-like pattern still dominates overall topology, with HGT producing localized reticulations. Keeling & Palmer (2008) noted that eukaryotic HGT is not so prevalent as to "undermine efforts to reconstruct a dichotomously branching tree."
- 发现 9: **Endosymbiotic gene transfer as macro-HGT.** In *Arabidopsis*, ~4,500 genes (~18%) are of cyanobacterial origin (Martin et al. 2002). Ongoing EGT is active: *Arabidopsis* nuclear genome contains a 262-kb insert 99.91% identical to mtDNA; rice nuclear genome contains a 131-kb complete chloroplast genome (99.77% identical). *Monocercomonoides* lost all mitochondria after HGT from bacteria provided replacement iron-sulfur cluster biogenesis genes — the first known eukaryote without any mitochondria-related organelle. [Ku et al. 2015](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4547308/ "PNAS 112:10139-10146") [Husnik & McCutcheon 2018](http://mccutcheonlab.org/s/17_husnik_nrm.pdf "citing Karnkowska et al. 2016, Current Biology 26:1274-1284")
- 发现 10: **The asexuality–HGT connection.** Bdelloid rotifers (obligately asexual) harbor 11.7–14.5% HGT genes — far exceeding any other protostome (max 3.6% in *Capitella teleta*). Desiccation-tolerant tardigrades and chironomids show only ~1% HGT, ruling out desiccation alone as the explanation (Nowell et al. 2018). Ku et al. (2015) proposed that in prokaryotes HGT serves the same function as sex — rescuing genomes from Muller's ratchet; in asexual bdelloid rotifers, elevated HGT may compensate for the loss of meiotic recombination. [Gladyshev et al. 2008](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/18511688/ "Science 320:1210-1213") [Nowell et al. 2018](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC5916493/ "PLoS Biology 16:e2004830") [Ku et al. 2015](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4547308/ "PNAS 112:10139-10146")
- 发现 11: **Eight functionally validated adaptive HGT events** (with experimental evidence): carotenoid biosynthesis in aphids (Moran & Jarvik 2010), courtship gene in Lepidoptera with CRISPR-confirmed 50% courtship reduction (Li et al. 2022), cdtB toxin in *Drosophila* (Verster et al. 2019), Fhb7 disease resistance in wheat (Wang et al. 2020), neochrome in ferns (Li et al. 2014), peptidoglycan-degrading enzymes limiting *Borrelia* in ticks (Chou et al. 2014), BtPMaT1 in whiteflies neutralizing plant defenses (Xia et al. 2021), and cell wall-degrading enzymes in herbivorous beetles (McKenna et al. 2019).
- 发现 12: **TE transfer predicts functional gene HGT.** Keeling & Palmer (2008) proposed that groups with frequent TE horizontal transfer likely also experience functional gene HGT "albeit at lower frequency," because the same ecological/biological conditions enable both. This prediction has been confirmed in insects, nematodes, and parasitic plants.
- 发现 13: **The 2025 reassessment reinforces the ratchet argument.** Even with ~70% of previously claimed plant iHGT events removed (Aguirre-Carvajal & Armijos-Jaramillo 2025), the remaining validated cases include transfers that fundamentally altered recipient biology — enabling parasitism (Cuscuta 108 genes), conferring disease resistance (Fhb7), and facilitating ecological diversification (neochrome). HGT is rare relative to prokaryotic HGT, but individual events that are retained can have transformative evolutionary consequences.
- 发现 14: **Inherited chimerism.** Ku et al. (2015) showed that mitochondrial and plastid endosymbionts brought "genome-sized samples" of ancestral prokaryotic pangenomes; subsequent prokaryotic recombination makes it impossible for eukaryotic EGT-derived genes to trace cleanly to a single modern prokaryotic lineage — explaining apparent phylogenetic incongruence without invoking multiple additional endosymbioses. [Ku et al. 2015](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4547308/ "PNAS 112:10139-10146")

## Chapter 6: Frontiers, Methodological Challenges, and Broader Implications
### 研究目标
- Major unresolved questions and methodological bottlenecks in eukaryotic HGT research.
- How long-read sequencing and pangenomics change the landscape.
- The contamination/artifact problem and maturing field standards.
- GMO biosafety angle: does natural HGT change how we evaluate transgene risk?
- Potential biotechnology applications inspired by natural HGT.
- Specific, actionable open research questions for the next decade.

### 关键发现
- 发现 1: **ContScout contamination detection tool.** Bálint et al. (2024) screened 844 published eukaryotic genomes and identified 51,222 contaminating proteins. Critically, ContScout flagged only 0–5.1% of literature-reported HGT genes as contamination, demonstrating the ability to distinguish genuine HGT from assembly artifacts. Contaminated data overestimated the LECA gene count by 21% (9,355 vs. 7,712 genes) and the Archaeplastida MRCA gene count by 88% (13,116 vs. 6,967 genes). [Bálint et al. 2024](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41467-024-45024-5 "Nature Communications 15:936")
- 发现 2: **Scale of database contamination.** Steinegger & Salzberg (2020) identified over 2 million contaminated entries in GenBank using Conterminator, demonstrating the scale of the contamination problem that confounds HGT detection in public sequence databases. [Steinegger & Salzberg 2020, cited in Bálint et al. 2024](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41467-024-45024-5 "Genome Biology 21:115")
- 发现 3: **First experimental recapitulation of HGT in animals.** Tarnopol et al. (2025) used genome editing to introduce phage-derived toxin genes (*cdtB* and *aip56*) — naturally horizontally transferred to *D. ananassae* ~21 Mya — into *D. melanogaster*. The *fusionB* chimeric gene, when expressed in immune tissues, promoted fly survival against parasitoid wasp *L. boulardi* (23.3% ± 10.0% vs. 3.7% ± 5.1% in controls, *p* = 0.00006) and reduced parasitoid emergence ~7-fold. [Tarnopol et al. 2025](https://www.cell.com/current-biology/fulltext/S0960-9822(24)01638-5 "Current Biology 35:514–528")
- 发现 4: **Constitutive HGT gene expression is lethal — regulatory domestication is essential.** Constitutive *fusionB* expression was lethal to *D. melanogaster*: pupariation delayed from 6.2 ± 0.53 to 9.86 ± 1.30 days (*p* < 2.2e−16) before catastrophic pupal arrest. This demonstrates that transferred genes must be carefully restricted to specific tissues to avoid autoimmunity. [Tarnopol et al. 2025](https://www.cell.com/current-biology/fulltext/S0960-9822(24)01638-5 "Current Biology 35:514–528")
- 发现 5: **euHGT dataset.** Spaulding & Fierst (2025) compiled 9,511 protein-coding HGT genes in metazoans from 36 published papers spanning 2000–2025, covering 13 phyla. Each gene carries a 1–5 certainty metric (level 5 = *in situ* hybridization + phylogenetics; level 4 = RNA expression + phylogenetics). The dataset addresses a critical bottleneck: researchers developing HGT identification methods have relied on simulated datasets due to the absence of empirical benchmark data. [Spaulding & Fierst 2025](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12637581/ "bioRxiv preprint, euHGT compendium")
- 发现 6: **Keeling (2024) theoretical reassessment.** Phagocytosis, while increasing exposure to foreign DNA, shifted eukaryotic adaptation toward cellular structure and behavior — variation "not readily available by HGT" — thus reducing, not increasing, functional HGT retention. Further, analyses of lineages with recent secondary/tertiary plastids showed "no evidence for an 'excess' of genes from the plastid lineage beyond organellar function genes," undermining use of genomic footprints as evidence for ancient endosymbioses. [Keeling 2024](https://www3.botany.ubc.ca/keeling/PDF/24HGT.pdf "Nature Reviews Genetics 25:416–430")
- 发现 7: **Long-read sequencing enables HGT boundary mapping.** McDonald et al. (2019) used long reads to confirm ToxA virulence gene cluster HGT in fungal wheat pathogens and precisely define transferred region boundaries — impossible with fragmented short-read assemblies. A 2025 ACM-BCB study proposed direct HGT identification from PacBio HiFi reads (>10 kb, 99.9% accuracy) bypassing assembly entirely. [cited in Pinto et al. 2022](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9471246/ "Frontiers Bioeng Biotechnol 10:971402") [ACM BCB 2025](https://dl.acm.org/doi/full/10.1145/3715020.3715045 "HGT from HiFi reads")
- 发现 8: **Pangenomics framework for HGT validation.** Sibbald et al. (2020) proposed that population-level resequencing can distinguish genuine HGT from ancestral polymorphism via presence/absence variation. Olofsson et al. (2019) showed in *Alloteropsis semialata* that an HGT-acquired gene spread through the population via positive selection on standing variation. [Sibbald et al. 2020](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S1471492220301975 "Trends in Parasitology 36:927–941") [Olofsson et al. 2019, cited in Keeling 2024](https://www3.botany.ubc.ca/keeling/PDF/24HGT.pdf "Current Biology 29:3921–3927")
- 发现 9: **Exponential growth in computational HGT tools, but AI adoption lags.** Wijaya et al. (2025) reviewed 100+ computational approaches (2000–2023): growth is exponential (*R²* = 0.998). Five AI-based tools exist (VirFinder, PhiSpy, RVM, Zhou et al. 2021 GCN, MSGIP) but all focus on prokaryotic genomic islands/prophages. No DL tool is specifically designed for eukaryotic nuclear HGT detection. A key barrier is the lack of standardized training datasets. [Wijaya et al. 2025](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11811736/ "NAR Genomics and Bioinformatics 7:lqaf005")
- 发现 10: **GMO biosafety: transgenic DNA is negligible vs. natural HGT rates.** Transgenic DNA in approved GM crops represents 0.00029–0.0017% of total plant DNA. Frequency of HGT from plant material to bacteria (7 × 10⁻²³ per cell without homology) is 10⁴–10¹⁴ times lower than naturally occurring bacterial HGT (10⁻¹ to 10⁻⁸ per cell). Over 30 naturally transgenic dicot species have been documented including peanuts, walnuts, tea, and banana. No adverse HGT events from GM plants have been reported across multi-generational feeding studies in Japanese quails, rats, broilers, pigs, and calves. [Pinto et al. 2022](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9471246/ "Frontiers Bioeng Biotechnol 10:971402")
- 发现 11: **Extracellular vesicle-mediated HGT demonstrated in parasitic protists.** Douanne et al. (2022) showed *Leishmania* parasites exchange drug-resistance genes through EVs. Fischer et al. (2016) reported DNA transfer between human cell lines via EVs. However, *in vivo* relevance for heritable HGT in multicellular organisms remains unestablished. [Douanne et al. 2022, cited in Keeling 2024](https://www3.botany.ubc.ca/keeling/PDF/24HGT.pdf "Cell Reports 40:111121") [Fischer et al. 2016](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC5042424/ "PLoS ONE 11:e0163665")
- 发现 12: **Vertebrate functional HGT: herring-to-smelt antifreeze protein.** Graham et al. (2008/2021) reported type II antifreeze protein gene transfer from herring to smelt, confirmed by accompanying TEs and broken gene synteny. Mechanism speculated: sperm-mediated uptake of environmental DNA during external fertilization. [Graham & Davies 2021](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0168952521000512 "Trends in Genetics 37:501–503")
- 发现 13: **IRBP: bacterial-origin key gene in vertebrate eye evolution.** Kalluraya et al. (2023) identified a bacterial origin for the IRBP gene involved in visual pigment regeneration — a rare bacteria-to-vertebrate functional HGT with major physiological impact. [Kalluraya et al. 2023, cited in Spaulding & Fierst 2025](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12637581/ "PNAS 120:e2214815120")
- 发现 14: **Keeling (2024) open questions for the next decade.** (1) Broader coverage of eukaryotic genomic diversity; (2) deeper coverage of closely related species for quantitative HGT rate estimation; (3) near-complete absence of mechanistic understanding — "we conclude HGT happened, and sometimes plausibly explain why, but we almost never know how"; (4) need for standardized methods enabling cross-lineage comparisons. [Keeling 2024](https://www3.botany.ubc.ca/keeling/PDF/24HGT.pdf "Nature Reviews Genetics 25:416–430")
